### Hard-cut rules (highest impact, lowest risk)
- **Delete “explaining sentences”** that restate what the reader already inferred.  
  - If a line starts with *“This meant…” “In other words…” “He realized…”* it’s usually cuttable.
- **Kill doubles**: keep the sharper of two similar beats.
  - “cold and icy” → “icy”
  - “stood up and rose” → “stood”
- **Convert 2–3 sentences into 1** when they’re one idea.
- **Remove staging micro-actions** unless they change meaning.
  - “He turned, walked, then looked” → “He looked.”
- **Cut most adverbs** (*really, suddenly, basically, actually*). They bloat and rarely add force.

### Compression swaps (keeps content, reduces characters)
- Replace phrasey stuff:
  - “in order to” → “to”
  - “due to the fact that” → “because”
  - “at this point in time” → “now”
  - “a large number of” → “many”
- Replace “there is/there are” sentences with direct subjects.
  - “There was a crack in the wall.” → “A crack split the wall.”

### Dialogue tightening (huge savings)
- If a line of dialogue only:
  1) repeats info, or  
  2) exists to transition (“so… anyway…”)  
  **cut it**.
- **Merge speaker tags + action**:
  - “he said” + separate action line → combine into one line.

### Description tightening (keep the image, cut the fog)
- Keep **one concrete sensory detail** per beat. Cut the rest.
- If you have 3 descriptors, keep 1–2:
  - “bright, clean, pinned where it couldn’t be missed” → pick best 1–2.

### A quick trick to hit your exact range
Once you’re close:
- If you’re **over 12k**, cut **every other** “thought sentence” (internal monologue tends to balloon).
- If you’re **under 10k**, add:
