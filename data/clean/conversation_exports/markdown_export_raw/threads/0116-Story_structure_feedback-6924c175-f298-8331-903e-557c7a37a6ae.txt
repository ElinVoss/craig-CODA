# TEO'GA: THE PIERCING EVENT
**Physical Reality, Planetary Impact, Permanent Consequences**

---

## DOCUMENT PURPOSE

This file defines the physical reality of the Piercing—the catastrophic event that begins the saga. Every geological, atmospheric, and structural consequence detailed here is **immutable canon** for all 30 novellas.

**Status:** LOCKED CANON — cannot be contradicted  
**Applies To:** All volumes, all timeline references  
**Cross-References:** 00-Saga-Blueprint.md, 02-Soulpulse-Core.md

---

## 1. THE PROJECTILE

### Physical Specifications

**Shape:** Elongated spindle, pointed on both ends

**Dimensions:**
- Length: 200 kilometers (approximately 180 bars)
- Maximum diameter: ~30 kilometers at center (approximately 27 bars)
- Tapers to sharp points at both ends

**Composition:**
- Fused glass-like material
- Sand and iron alloys
- Unknown metallic compounds (not matching any Te'Ogan elements)
- Surface coated with microscopic particulate matter (Soulpulse dust)

**Origin:** Alien — not created by any known culture on Te'Oga

**Behavior:**
- Travels in straight line through space
- Does not slow or deflect during planetary passage
- No deceleration upon impact or transit
- Clean pass-through — does not remain inside planet
- Continues into space after exit

## 1. COSMIC ORIGIN OF THE PRINCIPLES

Before time, form, or story, there was Counter-Spark—a pressure toward absolute stillness, wanting the universe as flawless, lifeless crystal.  
Into that total quiet came the First Spark, an unplanned "I AM" that ripped open the silence and allowed existence to begin.  

From that first wound of reality, four asymmetrical responses condensed into persons:  

- Xilcore — Form: structure, pattern, permanence  
- Leesa — Wholeness: unity, harmony, completion  
- Blemo — Purity: cleansing, refinement, clarity  
- Seeri — Entropy: decay, corrosion, necessary destruction  

They are not teammates; they are mutually incompatible absolutes locked in endless argument.  
Their ceaseless friction throws off the "psychic radiation" mortals later experience as Soulpulse, the background engine that makes life and change possible.  

Hidden law: a universe where any one Principle truly wins becomes frozen, empty, or collapsed; only their argument keeps anything moving.

**Counter-Spark, the Ancient War, and Te'Oga's Place in It**

## DOCUMENT PURPOSE

## 1. THE PRE-STORY UNIVERSE

### 1.1 Counter-Spark: Silence That Wants to Win

Before stars, time, or story, there is a pressure toward stillness: Counter-Spark.  
Its "desire" is simple—lock all potential into perfect, timeless, crystalline symmetry where nothing ever begins.  

Counter-Spark is not a god of death; death implies life first.  
It is a will to **no-story**: no change, no before/after, a flawless diagram that never gets built.  

### 1.2 First Spark and the Birth of Argument

The First Spark is an unplanned rupture: an "I AM" that tears a hole in that pressure and lets asymmetry into existence.  
From that wound, four incompatible answers to "what should existence be?" condense into the Divine Principles: Form, Wholeness, Purity, Entropy.  

- Form wants perfect structure.  
- Wholeness wants seamless harmony.  
- Purity wants flawless clarity.  
- Entropy wants all things to end.  

The universe is the space where these four argue—where their demands clash instead of resolving.  
Their friction produces a continuous background "soul-light" that fuels everything living, moving, and changing.

==== 50-Lexicon-Style.md ====
# 50 – LEXICON & STYLE GUIDE
**Prose Rules and Measurement Usage for Confluence Chronicles**

---

## 1. DOCUMENT PURPOSE

This guide defines how the story is written on the page: narrative voice, formatting, language constraints, and how the Te'Oga measurement systems are used in prose.  
It exists to keep all 30 novellas consistent in tone, clarity, and in-world terminology.

---

## 2. PROSE LAWS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

### 2.1 Point of View

- **Main chapters:** Close third person, anchored in the current POV character (usually Jhace or Tiffani in Turning 1, Jhace-focused later).  
- Camera stays inside their sensory and conceptual limits—no omniscient commentary in main chapters.  
- Interludes and epilogues are **Auditor-narrated** (see Section 6).

### 2.2 Tense

- Default: **tight past tense** for main chapters.  
- Short present-tense flashes allowed only for immediate, high-stress sensations (pain, impact, realization), and must be brief.

### 2.3 Language Constraints

- **No metaphors or similes** in main chapters.  
  - Do not write "like" / "as if" comparisons for imagery.  
  - Describe what is literally present: light, sound, motion, weight, temperature, stress.  
- Descriptions must be **plain, concrete, and physical**.  
  - "The beam bowed two fingers under the load" is correct.  
  - "The beam sagged like an old man" is not.

### 2.4 Magic Description (Grounded and Anime-Escalated)

- Soulpulse use is always described through **material effects and bodily cost**, not abstract energy:
  - Show beams straightening, glass knitting, joints locking, stone crumbling.  
  - Show shaking hands, shortness of breath, pain, exhaustion.

## 1. DOCUMENT PURPOSE

It exists to keep all 30 novellas consistent in tone, clarity, and in-world terminology.

## 2. PROSE LAWS (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

### 2.1 Point of View

- **Main chapters:** Close third person, anchored in the current POV character (usually Jhace or Tiffani in Turning 1, Jhace-focused later).  
- Camera stays inside their sensory and conceptual limits—no omniscient commentary in main chapters.  
- Interludes and epilogues are **Auditor-narrated** (see Section 6).

### 2.2 Tense

- Default: **tight past tense** for main chapters.  
- Short present-tense flashes allowed only for immediate, high-stress sensations (pain, impact, realization), and must be brief.

### 2.3 Language Constraints

- **No metaphors or similes** in main chapters.  
  - Do not write "like" / "as if" comparisons for imagery.  
  - Describe what is literally present: light, sound, motion, weight, temperature, stress.  
- Descriptions must be **plain, concrete, and physical**.  
  - "The beam bowed two fingers under the load" is correct.  
  - "The beam sagged like an old man" is not.

### 2.4 Magic Description (Grounded and Anime-Escalated)

- Soulpulse use is always described through **material effects and bodily cost**, not abstract energy:
  - Show beams straightening, glass knitting, joints locking, stone crumbling.  
  - Show shaking hands, shortness of breath, pain, exhaustion.  
- Even at late-saga anime levels:
  - No color-coded auras (no "blue aura of Purity," etc.).  
  - No vague "energy blasts" without specifying pressure, shrapnel, heat, concussion, or structural result.  
  - If someone "flies," it must be grounded (platforms of locked debris, wind pressure control, etc.).

## 4. CHARACTER KNOWLEDGE BOUNDARIES

When writing, respect what each character knows at each Turning (see 05-Knowledge-Gates.md).

- Jhace does not know Counter-Spark is the Auditor until T6.

**Soft boundaries (can be subtle):**

- Characters can suspect things without confirming them.

If a character seems to know too much too early, ask: "Would they have learned this by now?" If no, delay that knowledge to a later Turning or have them misunderstand/misconceive it.

## 5. COST ACCOUNTING

Every Soulpulse use has a cost. Track:

- **Immediate:** Tremor, exhaustion, pain in scene.
- **Session-level:** Carry forward fatigue state to next scene (if heavy use, character is compromised).
- **Long-term:** Chronic damage accumulates over saga (Jhace's tremors, for example).
- **Irreversible:** Death, permanent injury (rare, but real when it happens).

**Red flag:** If a character uses Soulpulse and shows no cost, fix it. Cost is not optional.

## 3. Real problems / contradictions / gaps

Here’s the part you actually care about.

### 3.1 Tiffani and resurrection

Right now, your **meta file hard-locks “no resurrection”**:

- `90-Meta-CommandLayer.md` explicitly says:
  - Tiffani is dead after N05; any presence is pattern/legacy only.  
  - ❌ “Resurrecting Tiffani” is listed as a red line.

If you’re still planning any late-book “Tiffani appears alive and executes an end-of-book reveal” style twist, this document is currently forbidding it.

You need to decide:

- Either:
  - Keep this red line, and commit 100% to “she never comes back as a conscious person,” only as pattern/legacy.  
- Or:
  - Redefine what you mean by resurrection (e.g., a Confluence construct that *looks* like her but is explicitly not a true return) and update the red-line language so you’re not contradicting yourself.

Right now, the docs and your previously stated intent are on a collision course.

### 3.2 Fathom Break definition is scattered

“Fathom Break” shows up across 5 files:

- As naming and timing (N05, N29)  
- As a cost level in the style doc  
- As a cosmological event in `04-Cosmology-Confluence.md`  
- As a knowledge gate in `05-Knowledge-Gates-Discovery.md`  

But there isn’t a single, tight “what exactly is a Fathom Break?” section with:

- Preconditions (what state Jhace has to be in).  
- What happens physically to:
  - Jhace’s body  
  - Local matter  
  - Global Soulpulse field  
- Permanent consequences:
  - For him (chronic damage, lifespan, pain profile).  
  - For the world (new coupling constant, gods unmasking, tier shifts).

This should live in `02-Soulpulse-Core.md` as a named subsection and then be referenced everywhere else. Right now it’s understandable, but you’re making future-you chase it across multiple files.

### 3.4 Year numbering is technically correct but slightly brain-bendy

You’ve got:

- “Total span: 6 years (6 Turnings × 1 year each).”  
- “Turning 1 (Year 0)… Turning 6 (Year 5).”

Mathematically fine (years 0–5 inclusive = 6 years), but it’s the kind of thing that can trip you in offhand notes or marketing copy.

Suggestion:

- Either:
  - Keep Year 0–5 internally but add a line saying “Externally we can just call this Years 1–6 if needed.”  
- Or:
  - Rename in-doc to Year 1–6 and call the Piercing “Year 1” instead of “Year 0.”  

Not a crisis, just something that will annoy you later if you’re tired.

### 3.6 One small potential misunderstanding trap: “no metaphors”

You did this smartly, but worth calling out:

- `50-Lexicon-Style.md` bans metaphors/similes in **main chapters**, then later explicitly allows them in **interludes/epilogues** via Auditor voice.

Functionally, that matches what you told me you wanted.

The only risk: if you forget that exception and start yelling at yourself for “breaking your own rule” in interludes. The doc itself is consistent, but future-you might misremember the nuance. You might want one explicit sentence high up:

- “Mainline Jhace/Tiffani chapters are literal-only. The Auditor gets the only license for figurative language.”

You already basically say this, but making it a bold single-line law wouldn’t hurt.
