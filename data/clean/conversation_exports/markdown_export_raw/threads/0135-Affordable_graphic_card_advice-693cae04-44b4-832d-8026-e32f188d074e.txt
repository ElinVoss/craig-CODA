Your i3-10100 is **PCIe 3.0** (so any modern GPU works), and 16GB RAM is fine. citeturn0search0  
The only real question is what *kind* of Lenovo you have (tiny/SFF tower vs normal tower), because that decides what cards physically fit and whether you have PCIe power cables.

products{"selections":[["turn1product0","Gigabyte GeForce GTX 1650 Low Profile 4GB (often no 6-pin)"],["turn0product0","ASRock Radeon RX 6400 Low Profile 4GB"],["turn1product1","MSI GeForce GTX 1050 Ti Low Profile 4GB"],["turn1product2","ASUS Phoenix GeForce GTX 1630 4GB"],["turn1product3","ASRock Intel Arc A380 6GB"],["turn0product1","MSI RTX 3050 6GB"],["turn0product2","MSI Radeon RX 6600 8GB"],["turn0product3","PNY RTX 4060 8GB"]],"tags":["Best slot-powered pick","Small case option","Cheapest decent option","Meh value","AV1/creator budget","Budget RTX features","Best 1080p value","Fast + efficient"]}

## If your Lenovo is **small form factor** (or PSU has **no 6/8-pin GPU power**)
Pick a **slot-powered** card (draws ≤75W from the motherboard):

- **GTX 1650 Low Profile (no 6-pin model)** = usually the best “drop-in and go” upgrade for these Lenovos. Note: some 1650 versions *do* require a 6-pin, so you must buy the **no-external-power** model. citeturn0search16  
- **RX 6400 Low Profile** = also slot-powered and fits SFF, but on your PCIe 3.0 system it can lose noticeable performance (it’s a PCIe x4 card). citeturn0search12turn0search18  
- **GTX 1050 Ti Low Profile** = works in almost anything, but it’s older/slower; only worth it if it’s dirt cheap.

## If your Lenovo is a **normal tower** with a decent PSU (and an 8-pin cable)
- **RX 6600** = best bang-for-buck for 1080p gaming in most cases.
- **RTX 4060** = costs more, but very power-efficient and generally stronger overall.

## If you care about **video/streaming/AV1 encoding** on a budget
- **Intel Arc A380** is often a great cheap “media” GPU, but it runs best with **Resizable BAR** enabled in BIOS (not strictly required, but strongly recommended). citeturn0search7  

## Tell me these 3 things and I’ll give you one exact pick
1) Exact Lenovo model (from the sticker: ThinkCentre/IdeaCentre + model code)  
2) Is it **small form factor** or normal tower?  
3) PSU wattage + whether you see a **6-pin or 8-pin PCIe** power plug inside the case
