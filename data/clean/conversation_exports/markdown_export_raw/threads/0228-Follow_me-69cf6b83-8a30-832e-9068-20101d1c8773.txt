**Cooldown (75–100%):** Timed platforms slow their cycle. One final dash orb into a clean landing. Music begins resolving.

### New Mechanic — Timed Platforms & Rotational Hazards
Timed platforms use the green/red/gone color cycle established clearly in the build-up. Rotational hazard corridors show their rotation direction with an arrow overlay that appears two beats before each turn — players learn to track arrow position as a secondary visual stream. Both mechanics teach *state reading* rather than *pattern memorization*.

### Skill Expression
A hidden alternate routing that uses only timed platforms — no dash orbs — through the peak section. Slower but requires tighter timing. Coin 2 requires waiting on a platform through one full retraction cycle, then re-extending cycle, which feels like a trust exercise. Coin 3 is placed inside a rotating corridor at the moment of rotation — only accessible by being in the correct lane one beat ahead.

### Dynamic Elements
The rotating ring background accelerates visually as the level speeds up, creating a pseudo-parallax speed effect. One "false alarm" rotation — the corridor arrow appears but the rotation doesn't happen, training players not to panic-react. A chasing energy ring begins following the player from behind in the peak section — no direct contact danger, but it creates psychological pressure and teaches pressure management for Level 7's actual chasing hazards.

### Visual & Audio Design
Track: fast electronic with a pulsing synth arpeggio that matches the timed platform cycle. The green/red/gone platform states are synced to the arpeggio's note pattern — experienced players will *hear* when to commit. The rotating ring background uses motion blur lines (not actual blur, just sharp angled stripes) to imply speed without visual noise.

### Difficulty Scaling
Consistent Insane through build-up, crosses into Easy Demon during the rotation + timed platform combination at peak. Expected clear: 80–200 attempts. This is the difficulty wall that separates casual players from committed ones.

### Replay Value
The alternate timed-platform routing is a genuinely different skill expression from the default path — players will want to master both. The psychological pressure of the "fake chase" prepares players for Level 7, making replays feel retroactively meaningful.

## LEVEL 6 — CHAIN REACTION
**Difficulty: Easy Demon / Medium Demon**

### Theme / Vibe
Orange and black. Explosive industrial energy — like a demolition sequence or a chain of controlled detonations. The background shows cascading shockwave rings each time the player lands on a platform. Satisfying to watch, intimidating to navigate. Rainbow Six Siege's breach-and-clear energy: move fast, read fast, commit.

### Core Gameplay Loop
**Spider mode** and **wave mode** enter. Spider mode teleports the player between floor and ceiling on a fixed track — one button, no analog. Wave mode is a continuous diagonal line where the player's height is directly controlled by how hard and fast they tap. Both modes demand micro-precision and introduce the last two vehicle types before the finale.

### Section Breakdown
**Intro (0–18%):** High-speed callback — cube/ship/dash sequence from Level 4's toolkit. No new mechanics yet. Establishes the tempo as the highest in the pack so far.

**Build-up (18–45%):** Spider mode section. Fixed corridors with timed platform elements from Level 5 — players must teleport between floor/ceiling to avoid gaps and spikes. Teaches discrete binary movement.

**Peak (45–70%):** Wave mode. A continuous narrow corridor with irregular wave-shaped walls. Players must maintain precise height control at high speed. The corridor widens on beats (safe to move), narrows off-beats (dangerous to move). Music sync is critical here.

**Transition (70–82%):** A full four-mechanic combined section — cube, gravity flip, dash, spider — cycling every four beats. A deliberate skills review before the finale.

**Cooldown (82–100%):** Long wave corridor, decelerating, clean exit.

### New Mechanic — Spider & Wave Modes
Spider: tapping flips between floor and ceiling teleportation along a visible track line. There's no airtime — it's instantaneous. This makes it more about rhythm than physics. Wave: the player's icon traces a diagonal line that rises when button is held and falls when released. In a narrow corridor, tiny inputs matter enormously. Both mechanics are introduced at moderate speed before hitting their peak difficulty.

### Skill Expression
Coin 1: requires entering an off-track spider detour that leads to a wider corridor with a hidden coin — but arriving late closes the gap. Coin 2: wave section, requires hitting a specific height for three consecutive beats with no deviation — pure muscle memory test. Coin 3: complete the four-mechanic transition section using only the alternate (no-dash) routing, which removes the safety buffer and requires tighter positional entry.

### Dynamic Elements
Shockwave rings cascade from the player's landing positions, creating genuine visual feedback for good landings and visual clutter for bad ones — players learn to read their own play quality from the environment. In wave mode, the walls glow brighter as the player gets closer to them — not a danger indicator, but a proximity warning that teaches spatial awareness before the visual cues become critical in Level 7.

### Visual & Audio Design
Track: heavy-hitting industrial hip-hop with an explosive snare on every peak beat. Spider teleport sounds like a compressed air burst. Wave mode has a continuous tone that pitches up as the player ascends and down as they descend — pure audio position feedback. Shockwave rings are purely visual and never overlap with spike hazards.

### Difficulty Scaling
Easy Demon through the build-up, crosses into Medium Demon during the wave + four-mechanic transition. Expected clear: 150–400 attempts. Players who've memorized the coin routes feel meaningfully ahead here.

### Replay Value
Wave mode is either the mechanic that "clicks" and becomes flow-state bliss, or the one that keeps players returning until it does. Either outcome drives strong replay. The four-mechanic transition section is the pack's clearest skills test and satisfying to execute cleanly.

## LEVEL 7 — KILL SWITCH
**Difficulty: Medium Demon**

### Theme / Vibe
White, red, and absolute black. Alarm-state aesthetic — the visual equivalent of an emergency klaxon. The background alternates between white flash and deep black in a strobe pattern synced tightly to the beat. This is the last level before the finale, and it should feel like a final exam. Siege operators executing a last-second defuse. Fortnite storm-circle closing with 10 seconds left.

### Core Gameplay Loop
**Chasing lasers** enter as the pack's signature dynamic hazard. These are tracking projectiles that pursue the player from behind and accelerate toward them if the player slows. Players must maintain momentum — stopping is death. Combined with every mechanic from Levels 1–6, this level demands that players internalize the full toolkit because they now have no margin to consciously process individual mechanics.

### Section Breakdown
**Intro (0–15%):** Speed opens at maximum. No ramp-up — this is a test, not a tutorial. Cube section with gravity flips at Level 4's peak complexity. The laser begins spawning at 12%, visible in the background before it becomes a real threat at 15%.

**Build-up (15–40%):** Laser is active and accelerating. Cube, spider, and wave sections in rotation — the laser pressure forces players to commit to every movement without hesitation. Hesitate on a spider flip and the laser catches up.

**Peak (40–70%):** Full dash-orb sequence across a corridor studded with timed platforms from Level 5, while the laser is at maximum acceleration. The corridor narrows mid-sequence. Visual strobe is at its highest frequency here — the level is designed to cause tunnel vision, training the player's focus to the immediate two seconds ahead.

**Transition (70–85%):** Laser briefly disappears — fake rest. Platform sequence resets. Laser returns at 82% faster and from closer range. The "false calm" is a deliberate mechanic teaching players not to relax until the victory screen.

**Cooldown / Finale (85–100%):** One final wave section, laser visible but distancing behind the player as they pull ahead. Ends with a clean exit. If the player clears this level, the pack "unlocks" Level 8 with a visual cutscene.

### New Mechanic — Chasing Lasers (Pseudo-AI Tracking)
Implemented using GD trigger logic: a moving object set to track the player's X position, with a constant acceleration value that increases by 15% every 10 seconds of level time. The laser starts slow enough to be ignorable. By the peak section, it's close enough that any pause longer than one beat means contact. It creates organic difficulty scaling without requiring the player to memorize its behavior — they *feel* it accelerating.

Implementation note (GD triggers): Use a **Move Trigger** on the laser object targeting the player group, with `center` targeting mode and a duration of 0.5s. A **Count Trigger** checks a timer item that increments via a **Spawn Trigger** every 10 seconds, each time reducing the Move Trigger's duration slightly. The laser is part of a Group ID that has collision detection against the player group.

### Skill Expression
Coin 1: deviate from the optimal laser-escape path into a slower route — requires enough of a speed lead on the laser to survive the extra frames. Coin 2: hidden inside the "false calm" section — only reachable while the laser is temporarily absent. Coin 3: complete the peak section without using any dash orbs (the safe routing), relying only on gravity flips and precise jumps.

### Dynamic Elements
Beyond the laser: wall segments slide in from both sides during the peak — same mechanic from Level 4 but at higher speed. Timed platforms now have a one-beat (rather than three-beat) cycle at peak — requiring prediction rather than observation. The strobe effect isn't decorative; it trains players to read hazards at low visual availability, mimicking the reduced information state of a competitive game's final seconds.

### Visual & Audio Design
Track: white-noise-heavy industrial with a pulse-alarm layered underneath. The kick drum is louder and sharper than previous levels. Laser approach is signaled by an audio pitch that rises as it gets closer — even with eyes locked on obstacles, players can hear it gaining. Strobe effect is implemented at a rate safe for photosensitive players (5–7 Hz maximum, not the epilepsy-risk 15+ Hz range). Clear visual warning is included at level select.

### Difficulty Scaling
Consistent Medium Demon throughout. No cooldown within the level until the true ending — this is intentional. Expected clear: 300–700 attempts. Players who've cleared Levels 1–6 with coins are meaningfully better prepared.

### Replay Value
The laser creates a genuinely different experience on every run because momentum management affects laser positioning. Two players who take identical paths can face different laser timing based on micro-hesitations. Organic variance drives organic replay.

## LEVEL 8 — APEX PROTOCOL
**Difficulty: Hard Demon ★ (Pack Finale)**

### Theme / Vibe
All colors. Pure white at maximum, deep black at minimum. The level opens in grayscale and progressively introduces every color from every previous level — cyan, purple, red, blue, green, orange, white — as each mechanic phase enters. By the finale, the screen is fully saturated. The message is clear: everything you learned is here. All at once.

### Core Gameplay Loop
Every mechanic from the pack, combined in rotating phases. No mechanic ever appears in isolation. The level is structured as a **three-phase boss fight** with distinct phases, a true "phase transition" visual break between each, and a boss-health-bar simulation showing % completion on screen.

---

### BOSS FIGHT STRUCTURE

**Phase 1 — "Boot Sequence" (0–33%)**
Callback to Level 1's Grid Pulse. Cyan and gray palette. The level opens at Level 1's speed — for exactly eight beats. Then it jumps to Level 7's speed instantly. The juxtaposition is deliberate: players who remember Level 1 feel the gap viscerally. From here: cube/ship/dash rotation, gravity flips, timed platforms, all at maximum Level 7 speed. The laser from Level 7 appears at 20% into Phase 1. No false calm.

**Phase 2 — "Override State" (33–66%)**
Colors shift to red and white. All colors of Levels 3–5. A second laser enters from *above*, tracking vertical position rather than horizontal — a variation that punishes players who learned to dodge only laterally. Wave mode section at the 45% mark — the narrowest corridor in the pack. Spider mode section at 55% — timed platforms cycle at one-beat speed. This phase deliberately prioritizes the mechanics that players found hardest in their individual levels.

Visual break: at 66%, the screen flashes white and holds for two beats — total whiteness. No danger during the flash. This is the only true rest point in Level 8. Players know it's coming after Levels 6–7 teach them not to trust false calmness.

**Phase 3 — "Apex State" (66–100%)**
All colors active simultaneously. Background is the layered composite of all seven previous levels' backgrounds, cycling at maximum speed. The laser from Phase 1 *and* Phase 2 are both active simultaneously. Players must manage both a horizontal and a vertical chaser. Speed is 10% above anything in the pack. The dash orb sequence from Level 4 appears in mirror — reversed left-to-right, requiring players to apply learned trajectories to unfamiliar geometry.

The final 10% is a single long wave corridor with no other hazards. Both lasers stop at the 92% mark. The player finishes alone, in wave mode, in pure white — a deliberate cooldown that rewards survival with peace.

### New Mechanics (Boss Unique)
**Dual laser tracking:** Horizontal laser from Level 7 + new vertical laser that tracks Y position. Vertical laser has slower base acceleration, giving players time to learn the additional threat without front-loading it.

**Mirror geometry:** Level 4's signature dash sequence mirrored. No new physics — entirely familiar mechanics in unfamiliar orientation. Tests genuine understanding rather than memorized pattern.

**Phase health bar:** Implemented via GD trigger: a static background object (the "health bar" visual) whose visible width is controlled by Move Triggers linked to the player's X-position progress. As the player advances, the bar shrinks. It's not interactive — purely visual — but creates a boss-fight psychological frame that fundamentally changes how the level feels.

### GD Trigger Implementation Notes
**Boss health bar:** Static wide rectangle positioned behind the UI layer. A `Move Trigger` set to `target player X` tracks progress and is multiplied into bar-width reduction via `Scale Trigger`. A `Toggle Trigger` switches the bar's color from green → yellow → red at the 33% and 66% marks, triggered by `Count Triggers` watching the player's checkpoint IDs.

**Dual laser system:** Two separate Group IDs for horizontal and vertical lasers. Each has its own `Move Trigger` chain with separate acceleration `Count Trigger` sequences. Vertical laser targets a player-group-offset Y value rather than the player's center to introduce slight tracking delay — prevents the vertical laser from being as punishing as the horizontal during simultaneous tracking.

**Phase transition flash:** A full-screen white rectangle with `Alpha Trigger` timed to 2 beats at the 66% mark. The `Spawn Trigger` at checkpoint 5 activates the flash and simultaneously activates Phase 3's background layer via `Toggle Trigger`.

**Color layer system:** Each phase's color palette is a separate background object group. `Toggle Triggers` at the phase boundaries enable/disable groups, creating the illusion of color "accumulation" in Phase 3.

### Section Breakdown
Phase 1 — callback recontextualization → laser entry.
Phase 2 — advanced combinations → two-laser introduction → true rest.
Phase 3 — all mechanics simultaneously → mirror sequence → dual laser finale → wave cooldown.

### Skill Expression
Three coins requiring completion of the *entire* corresponding previous level's signature sequence inside Level 8, at Level 8's speed and with lasers active. Coin 1: Level 3's triple gravity-flip sequence, here with both lasers active. Coin 2: Level 5's rotating-corridor section, here at Phase 2 speed. Coin 3: Level 4's dash sequence in *mirror form* — the hardest optional task in the pack, requiring true mechanical understanding rather than memory.

### Visual & Audio Design
Track: a composed mashup of all seven previous level tracks, layered in progressively — each phase adds another instrumental layer until Phase 3 is running all seven tracks simultaneously, creating a wall-of-sound that the music has been building to since Level 1. The final wave corridor plays only the Level 1 theme, solo and clean. Players who remember it from the start will feel the full arc.

### Difficulty Scaling
Opens at Hard equivalent (the Phase 1 "boot" callback), immediately jumps to Harder equivalent at the speed shift, reaches Medium Demon during Phase 2's two-laser section, and peaks at Hard Demon during Phase 3's simultaneous full-mechanic section. Never exceeds Hard Demon — everything is still readable, still fair, still based on mechanics the player has seen before. Unfair deaths are a design failure in this pack, not an accepted hardship.

### Replay Value
The highest in the pack. Every run is slightly different due to dual-laser positioning variance based on player micro-decisions. The mashup track rewards players who recognize each layer entering. The three coins are genuinely among the hardest optional tasks in any level pack — reachable but requiring dedicated practice. And completing Level 8 with all coins is a meaningful achievement: it means the player has mastered every mechanic in the pack at maximum difficulty, simultaneously, under pressure.

That's what Apex Protocol is. Not a level pack you finish. One you eventually solve.
