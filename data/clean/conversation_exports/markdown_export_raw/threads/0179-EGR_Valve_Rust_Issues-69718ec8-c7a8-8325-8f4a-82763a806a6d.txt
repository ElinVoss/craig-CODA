### What it most likely means (ranked)
1. **Coolant getting into the EGR stream**  
   If your setup has an EGR cooler (some variants do), a leaking cooler can put coolant vapor into the EGR path → rust + nasty deposits.  
   If there’s no cooler on your exact config, skip this.

2. **Condensation + short trips + clogged passages**  
   Lots of short-run driving = EGR stays cool, water condenses, mixes with soot → acidic paste → rusts springs/shafts.

3. **Water intrusion from intake side**  
   Flood event, pressure wash gone wrong, missing/compromised intake plumbing, or a PCV issue pushing a ton of moisture/oil vapor.

4. **The valve is stuck/slow and has been cooking itself**  
   Once it starts sticking, it stops moving properly, sits in hot crud, corrodes faster.

### What you should do now (practical steps)
- **Do not just slap it back on.** If the spring cavity is rusted, the valve can hang open/closed and cause rough idle, stalling, hesitation, pinging, and codes.
- **Check the EGR valve movement**: Does the pintle move smoothly? Any gritty spots or binding = replace.
- **Inspect the EGR passages** (intake/EGR port): if they’re coked tight, cleaning the valve alone won’t fix it.
- **Look for coolant evidence**:
  - Coolant level mysteriously dropping?
  - White crusty residue in EGR parts?
  - Sweet smell in exhaust?
  - Milky sludge anywhere it shouldn’t be?
- **Replace gaskets** no matter what. EGR gaskets don’t like being reused.

### Clean vs replace (how to decide fast)
Replace the EGR valve if:
- Spring/shaft is **pitted/flaking**
- Valve doesn’t return crisply
- Housing is rusted inside where the actuator rides
- You can feel sticking or hear crunch/grind
- You had EGR position/performance codes (P0401/P0402/P0403-type) or drivability issues

Cleaning is only worth trying if:
- Rust is mostly surface staining
- Movement is still smooth
- You can fully clean the bore/pintle and it still seals

**If it’s “rusty real bad,” I’d lean replace**. Rust in the actuator cavity tends to come back fast even after cleaning, and a sticking EGR can make the engine run like garbage.

## Safe-ish uses
- **External degreasing** (block, timing cover exterior, oil pan exterior, brackets)

- **Inside the intake while it’s still on the engine** where it can run into cylinders.
- **Anywhere near sensors/electronics** (MAP, TPS, connectors, actuator housings).

- **Top-end rust prevention/lube:** engine oil or assembly lube (ATF works in a pinch)
- **Cylinders/rings stuck from rust:** ATF or MMO soak (ATF + acetone if you want aggressive penetrant)
- **Carbon deposits (EGR/intake ports):** dedicated intake/EGR cleaner + nylon/brass brush
- **Final rinse:** brake cleaner (sparingly) + compressed air, then re-oil surfaces

- Diesel wash is fine, then dish soap + hot water, then dry completely.
If it’s still on:
- Don’t. Pull it. Otherwise you risk feeding diesel into cylinders and turning this into a bigger mess.

If you tell me what you’re trying to clean specifically (intake runners? valve train? cylinders?), I’ll tell you the exact solvent/process that won’t screw you.
