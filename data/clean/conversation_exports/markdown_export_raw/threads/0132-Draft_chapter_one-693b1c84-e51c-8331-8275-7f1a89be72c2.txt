### SAINT MERRO: CITY GEOGRAPHY

Saint Merro is a major urban center built in terraced layers up a hillside basin, with the river at the base and institutional districts at the peak.

**District Structure (Lowest to Highest):**

**The Dregs (River Level):**
- Poorest district, oldest construction
- Dense population, limited resources
- Most vulnerable to structural failure
- Industrial zones, slum housing
- Where refugees gather post‑Piercing
- Salee's community organizing happens here

**Underface Districts (Mid‑Level):**
- Working‑class residential and commercial
- Built partially into hillside, partially above ground
- Where most guild operations exist (Jhace's forge, craft shops)
- Heat‑grid maintenance tunnels run beneath these areas
- Mixed construction quality: some solid, much failing

**Glass Quarter (Upper‑Mid Level):**
- Precision craft district
- Merrow Glass & Mirrors located here
- Observatory tower at highest point of this district
- Better construction but still vulnerable
- Mixed workshops and wealthier residential

**The Crown (Peak):**
- Institutional and political center
- Best construction, most resources
- Least affected by Piercing initially
- Where council meetings and governance occur
- Where Corlexi works and institutional power concentrates

**The Underface Heat‑Grid:**
- Network of maintenance tunnels beneath working districts
- Copper piping carries geothermal heat from deeper sources
- Critical infrastructure for city heating and some industrial processes
- Narrow passages, vertical risers, humid, constant rumble
- Where Jhace spends much of his working time

### THE PIERCING: WHAT HAPPENED

**Tide 0, Kindling Phase:** A colossal, double‑tapered shard struck Te'Oga. It did not skim the atmosphere or glance off the surface; it punched straight through the planet.

**Physical Characteristics (As Observed):**
- Overall form: a spear‑like cylinder, tapered to points at both ends
- Apparent scale: planetary, spanning from entry horizon to exit horizon
- Composition (visual): strata of translucent glass‑like material, dark iron bands, unknown alloys, embedded flecks and veins the color of dried blood
- Surface behavior: edges glowed white‑hot on entry, cooling to copper‑gold embers as it passed

**Trajectory and Interaction with Te'Oga:**
- Entry from high heaven at a steep, impossible angle
- Direct penetration through mantle and core
- Exit on the opposite side of the world, leaving a cylindrical bore through Te'Oga's body
- Object did not stop or lodge; it continued into deep space, trailing particulate and vaporized matter

**Immediate Planetary Effects:**
- Axis shift: Te'Oga's rotational tilt snapped toward near‑upright in a single catastrophic adjustment
- Global quakes: crust‑rending shocks propagated along every plate line, collapsing marginal structures across all regions
- Ocean displacement: seas heaved toward the new bore; coastlines experienced retreat followed by return walls of water
- Atmospheric violence: compression fronts raced ahead of the impact path, shredding roofs, towers, and exposed spans

**The Permanent Wound:**
- A world‑spanning, near‑vertical tunnel remains where the shard passed
- Rim regions around the entry and exit wounds suffer constant erosion, collapse, and hydrological instability
- Water, silt, and atmospheric gases continue to pour into the bore, cycling through pressure and phase changes in depths no one has yet measured

**Consequences Relevant to N01:**
- Saint Merro sits within the primary seismic influence of the passage
- Its heat‑grid, terrace foundations, and hillside retaining structures were all stressed beyond their intended tolerances
- The city's survival depends on constant maintenance, emergency reinforcement, and improvised engineering under conditions no guild ever trained for

### WORLD STATE AT THE START OF N01

**Pre‑Piercing Baseline:**  Saint Merro is a terraced city with a functional guild system.  Higher districts are wealthier and more stable; lower districts are older and fragile. **The Piercing Event (Tide 0):**  A massive cylindrical object enters the atmosphere at an impossible angle and speeds, warping light as it passes.  It shears across the sky without touching the ground and continues into space.  Shockwaves shatter structures; dust and metallic particles rain down.  Many buildings collapse; hundreds die.  Traditional physics fails at the quantum level.  This is the inciting incident of the entire saga.

**Post‑Piercing Conditions:**  The city is partially destroyed.  Institutions are overwhelmed.  Resources are scarce.  People are traumatized.  New abilities (Soulpulse) begin to emerge in roughly 1 in 500 individuals.  The heat‑grid, critical to winter survival, is failing.  Guilds fracture over how to respond.  The Regulatory and Reconstruction Office (RRO) forms to coordinate relief but quickly drifts toward control and bureaucracy.

### JHACE & TIFFANI – THE CORE PARTNERSHIP

This is the emotional spine of N01.  They move from strangers with misconceptions to partners who know they can’t survive the crisis without each other.

#### Phase 1: Mutual Judgment (Ch 1–3)

* **Jhace’s view:** Tiffani is the perfectionist who prevents work.  Guild gossip paints her as someone who refuses commissions if specifications aren’t perfect, causing projects to stall.  He assumes that sort of person will get good workers killed by refusing to act.
* **Tiffani’s view:** Jhace is the overextending hero who thinks only he can save people.  She believes he takes on too much out of ego.  From a distance it looks like savior complex disguised as quality control.
* **Reality:** Both are wrong.  They haven’t met yet and are projecting their own fears.  This phase ends when the Piercing forces their paths to cross.

#### Phase 2: Competence Recognition (Ch 4–7)

* **First contact (Ch 3):** Tiffani sees Jhace holding a collapsing building.  Her first reaction isn’t awe but analytical curiosity.  “What did you do?” matters more than “how brave.”
* **His recognition:** During the major hold in Ch 6, Tiffani’s critique (“You’re applying force uniformly when the load is uneven”) annoys him until he realizes she’s right.  It wasn’t judgment; it was insight that saves lives.
* **Her recognition:** In Ch 7, she sees how exhausted and shaken he is after each use.  She realizes his obsessive vigilance mirrors her own fear of missed patterns.  They’re both serving the same moral imperative with different tools.
* **Status at the end of Phase 2:** They respect each other professionally.  Partnership is brewing but not yet named.  Care emerges as Tiffani catches him when he collapses and he insists she rest her injured leg.

#### Phase 3: Partnership Formation (Ch 8–11)

* **Trust building:** They integrate their efforts.  He allows her to guide where he applies his power; she modifies her analytical approach based on what his body can handle.  They decide resource priorities together.
* **Conflict:** They have their first methodological fight in Ch 9 over how to train Kylar.  She wants slower emotional recovery; he wants immediate practice to rebuild confidence.  Asking Kylar what he needs resolves the fight and shows they can disagree without breaking trust.
* **Physical intimacy emerges:** Not yet sexual.  Touch now includes brushing fingers, sitting shoulder to shoulder, passing cups during tremor.  Their bodies learn to relax around each other.
* **Political alignment:** In Ch 11 they stand together against Corlexi’s registration demand.  It’s their first united front to an external authority.

#### Phase 4: Romantic Recognition (Ch 12–15)

* **The shift:** They both realize the partnership is more than professional.  Small non‑work gestures accumulate into undeniable feelings.  Silence becomes comfortable rather than loaded.
* **Acknowledgment scene (Ch 13):** After a long day, she admits “This is more than work partnership, isn’t it?”  He replies “Yeah.”  That is their declaration.  They acknowledge romantic attraction without drama.
* **Physical escalation:** They hold each other after crises, share body heat while sitting, enjoy the closeness.  Tiffani begins making surgical sex jokes directed at him (see Character Dossiers) to lighten tension.  They avoid consummation only because they’re physically exhausted.
* **Commitment scene (Ch 15):** They sit in a quiet space and decide to keep holding together.  “The world is broken.  We can’t fix all of it.  But we can hold something.”  “Then we hold.”  No formal vows; this promise is enough.

### UNDERFACE HEAT‑GRID TUNNELS

**Purpose:** Maintenance access for the city’s heating infrastructure.

**Physical Layout:**

* A network of narrow passages beneath the working‑class districts of Saint Merro.
* Copper pipes of varying diameters carry geothermal heat; smaller lines (thimble‑width) snake over large main lines (pole‑width).
* Vertical risers connect deeper levels via ladders; some spaces require squeezing through tight shaft openings.
* The tunnels are single file in most places, with slightly wider junctions for maintenance crews and tool caches.
* Multiple access points: surface grates, doors at forge‑level basements, emergency exits into the Dregs and Underface districts.

**Environmental:**

* Heat: Temperature rises with depth – warm near the surface, becoming uncomfortable near deep mains.
* Humidity: Condensation drips down walls and pipes; occasional puddles gather on the stone floor.
* Sound: Constant low rumble from heat flow, clangs as pipes shift; conversations echo unpredictably and aren’t private.
* Light: Limited to carried lamps or fixed lanterns at junctions; shadows dance on curved walls.

**Signature Visuals:**

* Copper pipes shimmering with heat distortion.
* Chalk marks noting maintenance or structural warnings.
* Tool racks (wrenches, hammers, spare flanges) at intersections.
* Steam vents hissing gently along some runs.

**Functional Details:**

* The tunnels supply heat to homes, guild halls and industrial sites. Failures can mean freezing residents or catastrophic pressure releases.
* Jhace’s inspection routes run through these tunnels; he feels for vibration changes and listens for subtle hum shifts to detect problems.
* Post‑Piercing, the heat‑grid is under unusual strain – more leaks, more pressure spikes, more points of imminent failure.

**Typical Scenes:**

* Chapter 1 opens here with Jhace inspecting the grid and feeling something wrong.
* Chapter 3 shows the tunnels rattling and rumbling during the Piercing.
* Subsequent chapters (4, 6, 12) use them as shelters, shortcuts and sites of quiet reflection or crisis navigation.

**Travel Time:**

* From street level to inspection depth: a few spans of climbing/descending, depending on access point.
* Cross‑district travel underground can be faster than surface routes during crises but is disorienting without experience.

**Purpose:** Lay down the rules that govern language, magic costs, knowledge and structure in N01. These are mechanical constraints the prose engine must obey to preserve immersion, mystery and pacing.  Violating them breaks the story.

### 1. LEXICON ENFORCEMENT

**No Earth terms for time, distance, direction, measurement or culture.**  Te’Oga has its own units.  If any of the forbidden terms appear in prose, the validator must flag them and the draft must be revised.

#### Forbidden Time Terms

* Day, week, month, year, season names (spring, summer, fall/autumn, winter).
* Days of week (Monday, Tuesday, etc.).
* Hour, minute, second, o’clock, AM, PM, midnight, noon.

#### Forbidden Distance/Direction Terms

* Foot, feet, inch, yard, mile, meter, kilometre, centimetre.
* North, south, east, west, NE, NW, SE, SW, compass.

#### Forbidden Measurement Terms

* Pound, ounce, ton, tonne, gram, kilogram.
* Gallon, quart, pint, litre, ml.
* Celsius, Fahrenheit, degrees.

#### Forbidden Modern Slang and Culture

* “Okay,” “OK,” “cool,” “awesome,” “dude,” “guy” when used as slang or filler.
* Any real‑world religions, mythologies, historical events, or geographical names.

#### Required Te’Oga Terms

* **Time:** Tide (~24 hours), Anneal (~5 Tides), Crucible (~35 Tides), Turn (personal age), Cast (historical epoch).  Within a Tide: Kindling, Pouring, Shaping, Striking, Quench.
* **Short Duration:** Span (seconds to minutes), breath (moment), heartbeat (instant).
* **Distance:** Span (arm’s length), pace (step), pole (~10 feet).
* **Direction:** Up‑slope (toward the Crown), down‑slope (toward the river), harbor‑side, inland, or landmarks.
* **Volume:** Thimble (small), cup (container), cask (large).
* **Weight:** Grain (tiny), stone (heavy), weight (formal unit).

#### Acceptable Substitutions

* “Yes,” “understood,” “fine,” “agreed” instead of “OK/cool/awesome.”
* “Person,” “man,” “woman,” “worker,” “apprentice” instead of modern slang like “dude” or “guy.”

## SOULPULSE: MECHANICS AS DISCOVERED IN N01

**Purpose:** This document describes Soulpulse as it's understood and experienced during N01 "The Grip That Holds." This is empirical knowledge built from observation and experimentation, NOT complete system understanding.

---

### WHAT IS SOULPULSE?

**Observed Phenomenon:**
Following the Piercing, certain individuals discovered they could affect physical reality through focused intention combined with direct contact. This wasn't magic in any traditional sense—no incantations, no reagents, no mystical gestures.

**Common Element:**
Protective intention crystallized through physical contact. The user must desperately need the material to hold/mend/stabilize AND must be touching it.

---

### ACTIVATION REQUIREMENTS (As Understood in N01)

**Physical Contact:**

## BEAT CATALOG — N01 SCOPE

**Purpose:**  
Define all beat methods used in N01 so chapters can be:

- Planned and validated structurally.  
- Kept unpredictable at the series level.  
- Emotionally coherent within themselves.

N01 uses **eight** beat methods: BEAT_01 through BEAT_08.

---

### BEAT_01 — Observation → Pattern Recognition

**Function:**  
Use when the chapter is about **noticing something wrong** and understanding it.

**Structure:**

1. **Baseline Observation**  
   - POV engages in normal work/routine.  
   - Small detail feels off (vibration, light behavior, behavior pattern).

2. **Focused Attention**  
   - POV chooses to pay attention instead of ignoring.  
   - Compares against prior experience.

3. **Testing / Probing**  
   - Small actions to test the anomaly.  
   - Additional observations confirm the anomaly is real.

4. **Pattern Recognition**  
   - Click moment: “This is not random.”  
   - POV understands the pattern (or understands that a pattern exists and is dangerous).

5. **Decision / Micro-Shift**  
   - POV changes course or plan based on the new understanding.

**Emotional Arc:**  
Curiosity → Unease → Focus → Understanding → Resolve

**Best Used When:**
- Introducing a core problem.  
- Showing a character’s core mode of thinking (Jhace sensing structures, Tiffani reading patterns).

**Forbidden Sequences:**
- Cannot immediately follow another BEAT_01.  
- Cannot immediately precede BEAT_06 (both are recognition/synthesis-heavy).

**N01 Examples (intended):**
- Ch 1 (Jhace: “Something Wrong” under the heat-grid).  
- Ch 2 (Tiffani: observing upper atmosphere anomalies).

---

### BEAT_02 — Trial → Failure → Compounding → Success/Cost

**Function:**  
Use when you want a **hard failure** that escalates, followed by a victory that hurts.

**Structure:**

1. **Trial Setup**  
   - Goal is defined (save someone, stabilize structure, train Soulpulse).  
   - Plan seems plausible but not guaranteed.

2. **Initial Attempt → Failure**  
   - First attempt fails clearly.  
   - Stakes increase.

3. **Compounding Problem**  
   - Failure makes situation worse (more damage, less time, more people at risk).

4. **Reframe / New Approach**  
   - POV changes approach, often with help from another character.

5. **Success with Explicit Cost**  
   - Goal is achieved or partially achieved.  
   - Cost is visible: physical, emotional, moral, or relational.

**Emotional Arc:**  
Hope → Frustration → Panic → Determination → Relief + Dread

**Best Used When:**
- Training episodes that go wrong.  
- Major rescue that almost fails.  
- Moments where price of success must be undeniable.

**Forbidden Sequences:**
- Cannot follow another BEAT_02.  
- Should not directly precede BEAT_03 in most cases (risk of fatigue).

**N01 Examples (intended):**
- Kylar’s first training attempt + follow-up.  
- A rescue where Jhace’s first plan fails, and Tiffani’s input salvages it.

---

### BEAT_03 — Crisis Front-Loaded → Rolling Complications → Partial Resolution

**Function:**  
Use for big chaos chapters, especially disasters in progress.

**Structure:**

1. **Drop-In Crisis**  
   - Chapter opens mid-disaster: building already collapsing, Piercing underway, etc.

2. **Rolling Complications**  
   - Every attempt to stabilize reveals new problems.  
   - Stakes grow organically (more people, more structure at risk).

3. **Local Focus Choice**  
   - POV must choose where to focus attention.  
   - Implicit acceptance that some things will be left to fail.

4. **Partial Resolution**  
   - Immediate crisis is stabilized enough to survive.  
   - Larger structural problem remains unsolved.

5. **Fallout Preview**  
   - Hints at future consequences for this imperfect solution.

**Emotional Arc:**  
Shock → Scramble → Triage → Exhausted Survival → Uneasy Aftermath

**Best Used When:**
- Piercing event itself.  
- Major structural disaster chapters.  
- Big pivot moments in the novella.

**Forbidden Sequences:**
- Cannot be used more than 3 times in N01.  
- Never back-to-back.  
- Must be buffered by lower-intensity beats.

**N01 Examples (intended):**
- Ch 3 (The Piercing).  
- Ch 6 (major hold).  
- Ch 14 (district-spanning crisis).

## N01 VALIDATOR CHECKLIST

**Purpose:**  
Provide a strict, mechanical validation pass for any N01 prose (chapter, Interlude, or Epilogue) to enforce:

- Lexicon purity.  
- Soulpulse cost visibility.  
- Voice and POV discipline.

This checklist is intended to be run **after** a draft is written or revised.

### 1. LEXICON ENFORCEMENT

#### 1.1 Forbidden Time Terms

Search the prose for each of the following; if any appear, they must be replaced:

- day / days  
- week / weeks  
- month / months  
- year / years  
- season / spring / summer / fall / autumn / winter  
- Monday / Tuesday / Wednesday / Thursday / Friday / Saturday / Sunday  
- hour / minute / second  
- AM / PM / o'clock / midnight / noon

For each hit, confirm there is a correct substitution:

- Tide, Anneal, Crucible, Turn, Cast, Span, Breath, Heartbeat, phase names (Kindling, Pouring, Shaping, Striking, Quench).

#### 1.2 Forbidden Distance / Direction Terms

Search and eliminate:

- foot / feet / ft  
- inch / inches / in  
- yard / yards  
- mile / miles  
- meter / metre / m / kilometer / km / centimeter / cm  
- north / south / east / west / NE / NW / SE / SW / compass

Confirm use of:

- Span, Pace, Pole, upslope/downslope, river-side/harbor-side/inland, landmark-based directions.

#### 1.3 Forbidden Measurement Terms

Search and eliminate:

- pound / lb / lbs / ounce / oz / ton / tonne  
- gram / g / kilogram / kg  
- gallon / quart / liter / litre / ml  
- Celsius / Fahrenheit / °C / °F / degree / degrees

Confirm weights/volumes are expressed via:

#### 1.4 Modern Slang and Cultural Leakage

Search and eliminate:

- okay / OK / ok  
- cool / awesome / dude / guy (as generic modern slang)  
- any mention of real-world religions, mythologies, countries, historical events, or technologies that do not exist in Te’Oga.

Confirm substitutions:

- “Yes,” “Understood,” “Agreed,” “Fine,” etc., instead of “okay / cool / awesome.”

---

### 2. SOULPULSE COST AND MECHANICS

For every Soulpulse activation in the text:

#### 2.1 Activation Requirements

- Is the user in **direct contact** with the material?  
- Is their **emotional state** clearly protective/desperate rather than casual?  
- Is it clear **what** they are trying to hold/stabilize/mend?

If any of these are missing, revise.

#### 2.2 During-Use Description

- Copper-gold glow is explicitly described.  
- Glow spread pattern into material is described (even briefly).  
- Material response is visible (shimmer, tension, redistribution).  
- Physical strain in the user is shown (tremor, muscle tension, breath changes).

If an activation reads “he used his power and the beam held” with no detail, it fails.

#### 2.3 Immediate Aftermath

- Tremor status is shown (worse / same / slightly improved).  
- Vein-line visibility is described (bright, fading, faintly present at rest).  
- Exhaustion is concrete (struggling to lift something, dizziness, needing support).  
- Pain is localized and characterized (hands, arms, chest; burning, aching, stabbing, etc.).
