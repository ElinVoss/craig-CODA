### 1. What the hell is an “exchange”?

Think of an exchange like:

- A **crypto pawn shop / bank hybrid** where:
  - You deposit money (cash or crypto)
  - You trade it for other coins or back to dollars
  - They hold your balance in an account

Examples (just so you have names in your head): Coinbase, Kraken, Binance, etc.

You log in → see a balance → click buttons to buy/sell. That’s it on the surface.

### 2. Where your $50 PYUSD probably is

Right now your $50 PYUSD is most likely in one of these:

1. **PayPal/Venmo app**
   - Then PayPal is acting as the “exchange” for you.
   - You don’t see blockchains, wallets, or keys. It’s just numbers on a screen.

2. **A centralized exchange (CEX)** like Coinbase, Kraken, etc.
   - Same idea: you see a balance on their app/website.
   - They control the underlying crypto; you just have an IOU on their system.

3. **Your own wallet** (Metamask, Phantom, Ledger, etc.)
   - Then *you* are the bank.
   - You hold the private keys; nobody can touch it unless they get your seed phrase.

Since you said you “know nothing of exchange,” you’re probably in bucket 1 or 2, not self-custody.
