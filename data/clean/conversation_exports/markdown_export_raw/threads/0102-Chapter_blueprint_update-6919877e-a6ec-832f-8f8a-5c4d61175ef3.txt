## **ACT I: NORMALCY**

### **CHAPTER 1: The Grid — Jhace POV**
**~1,200 words | Morning, Day of Piercing**

**Opening:** Jhace descends into the Underface with Bram at first light for routine inspection. The grid hums steady—throat-sung true—as it has every watch for years. Chalk dust coats his hands; the smell of limestone and old heat fills his lungs.

**World-Building:** The Underface is Te'Oga's lifeline: a network of heat risers, copper veins, and iron ribs that carries warmth from the geothermal core through the vertical city. Jhace's job is to listen—to read appetite (the metal's hunger to fail) before it speaks loud enough to kill someone.

**Craft Display:** At Junction 2-nine, Jhace pauses. Bram sees nothing wrong. Jhace places his bare palm on a saddle pin and feels it—a sourness, a quarter-turn too loose. He corrects it before failure, marks it with chalk (X-in-circle = done). Bram watches, half-admiring, half-worried. "You feel too much," he says. Jhace doesn't answer. Feeling is the only thing he trusts.

**Character Moment:** Bram tries to pull Jhace into conversation about isolation, about finding people instead of metal. Jhace deflects: "Metal doesn't leave. People do." The words are a wall, but they're cracked. He lost his parents to a collapse five years ago—suffocated in rubble while he was three districts away. He's never forgiven himself for not being there. For not *feeling* the wrongness in time.

**Foreshadowing:** As they finish the circuit, Jhace hears it: a whine, high and thin, threading down through stone. He stops mid-step. Bram hears nothing. Jhace presses his ear to a riser—nothing he can name, but it's *there*. The grid hums a hair off-key, like a string tuned wrong. He logs it mentally but doesn't know what to call it.

**Closing Image:** Jhace stands alone in the corridor after Bram leaves for surface duty. He rests his forehead against cold iron, closes his eyes, and *listens*. The whine grows. His jaw buzzes. Something is coming, and the grid knows it before he does.

**Emotional Beat:** Loneliness worn like armor; competence as coping mechanism; grief as background hum.

### **CHAPTER 2: The Measure — Tiffani POV**
**~1,200 words | Morning, Day of Piercing (parallel to Ch1)**

**Opening:** Tiffani stands in the Glass Quarter observatory—a vaulted glass-house three stories above the market—measuring horizon deflection with her angle-cage. The device is brass and precision: plumb-weights, sighting frames, bubble levels. She records deflection to three decimal places. Mikel, her apprentice, watches with the patient confusion of someone who hasn't yet learned that numbers don't lie.

**World-Building:** The Glass Quarter is where Te'Oga's geometric work happens: proof-walls (thick observation glass), casting platforms, measurement stations. Tiffani's mentor, Marisse, taught her that *precision is mercy*—that structures fail because people guess instead of measure. Marisse died two years ago when a span she didn't calculate herself collapsed under Crown-rushed construction. Tiffani has trusted only her own numbers since.

**Craft Display:** Tiffani uses parallax sighting to measure the eastern horizon. It should be flat within tolerance. It's not. The data shows a systematic inward bow of 1.2 fingers over a three-span baseline. She rechecks. Same result. Mikel asks if it's instrument error. Tiffani's answer is clinical: "Systematic means it is real. Solving comes after knowing."

**Character Moment:** Mikel tries small talk—asks about her weekend, about the market festival. Tiffani's responses are polite but clipped. She doesn't do small talk; it's imprecise, inefficient. Mikel looks hurt. Tiffani notices, files it under *costs of clarity*, and returns to her sighting frame. She knows she's difficult. She prefers it to being wrong.

**Foreshadowing:** As she logs the final measurement, the eastern sky shimmers—a heat-distortion effect that shouldn't exist in cool morning air. She blinks. It's gone. She records it anyway: *Visual aberration, 0847, duration <2sec, cause unknown.* Mikel didn't see it. Tiffani trusts her eyes more than his.

**Closing Image:** Tiffani closes her logbook and stands at the proof-wall, staring east. The shimmer returns—brief, like light bending through warped glass. Her pulse quickens. She doesn't know what she's seeing, but her body does. The geometry of the world is wrong, and wrongness has a shape. She just hasn't named it yet.

## **ACT II: CATALYST**

### **CHAPTER 3: The Piercing — Jhace POV**
**~1,500 words | Midday, Day of Piercing**

**Opening:** The whine becomes a bellow. Jhace is mid-inspection with Bram when the sound drops into his bones—so deep it's not sound anymore, just pressure. The torque key jumps in his hand. Dust rises from floor seams. Bram's eyes go wide.

**The Event:** A white-hot line slashes across the proof-wall view—too straight, too fast, impossibly bright. It doesn't move *through* the sky; it *cuts* it. Clouds convulse. Light buckles. The line thickens, shouldering reality aside like a blade through fabric. Jhace feels gravity *loosen*—his boots lift a fraction, weight sliding sideways instead of down. The Underface groans: metal screaming, stone cracking, the grid's hum skipping like a broken heartbeat.

**The Run:** Jhace doesn't think. He runs. Toward the screaming. Toward the burned-glass smell threading down through shattered corridors. Bram shouts his name; Jhace doesn't stop. He knows collapse when he feels it—this is *big*. This is Glass Quarter.

**The Rescue:** He bursts into the workfloor and sees hell: vaulted ceiling torn open, proof-wall shattered, casting platform buckled. A beam the size of a tree has fallen, pinning someone beneath it. Jhace sees pale hands first—fingers locked under a crossbar, trying to lift the world. Then her: leg trapped, molten glass cooling over exposed skin in a cracked shell that still glows orange from within. She's not screaming. Her jaw is clenched, eyes bright with fury. "Either run or help," she rasps.

**First SoulPulse:** Jhace strips off his glove and puts his bare hand on the glass. Heat bites—then *floods*. Not surface burn: something deeper, like fire crawling into his veins and *staying*. He doesn't pull away. He *pulls the heat in*, instinctively, the way you pull a splinter. The glass cracks under his palm, cools enough to move. A worker jams a lever under the beam. Jhace heaves; metal shifts. She jerks her leg free, tearing the glass shell. The sound she makes isn't quite a scream—it's rage and relief in one breath.

**The Change:** Jhace lets go. His palm comes away sticky, skin torn where it stuck to cooling glass. He doesn't look at it—doesn't want to see. But he *feels* it: something threading under his skin, a warmth that wasn't there before, writing itself into his bones. His hand throbs, but not like a burn. Like a *change*.

**Closing Image:** The woman—Tiffani, he'll learn later—looks at him with sweat-streaked face and glass dust in her hair. "You didn't know me," she says, voice hoarse. "Why?" Jhace doesn't have an answer. He just wanted one thing to not fall. For once. She holds his eyes for a beat, then limps away, leaning on a worker's shoulder. Jhace looks at his palm. A thin, dark line threads from wrist-bone toward thumb. It wasn't there before. The world has changed. So has he.

**Emotional Beat:** Instinct over thought; heroism as grief response; the moment magic becomes real.

The workfloor is visible through the warehouse door: vaulted ceiling torn like paper, proof-walls in glittering drifts across buckled deck. **The Awakening:** She tries to stand, using a support beam. Her vision doubles—then *clarifies*. Blue lines write themselves across the wreckage: load paths, stress vectors, structural appetite mapped in geometry she can *see* instead of calculate. She gasps. The lines pulse softly, showing where the collapse wants to continue, where it's held by accident, where intervention would matter. This isn't measurement. This is *sight*.

**The Man:** She finds him sitting against a wall, hand wrapped in someone's torn shirt. He's staring at nothing, jaw tight. She recognizes him: Underface worker, the one who ran *toward* disaster. **Bram's Warning:** Bram finds them in the yard, looks at Jhace's ledger, and says the thing they're both thinking: "You're spending yourselves like coin. What happens when you run out?" Jhace doesn't answer. Tiffani does: "Then someone else spends themselves. Or the city falls."

**The Math:** They calculate: if vein expands 0.5 per major repair, Jhace has maybe 15-20 repairs before he hits critical (vein 5.0+, function loss). Tiffani has maybe 20-25 holds before hands stiffen beyond use. They look at each other, do the darker math: Te'Oga needs hundreds of repairs. They can't do it alone. No one can.

**The Pact:** They make it formal, in Bram's presence as witness. Rules: No work past vein 3.0 or hands 80%. No solo heroics. Costs logged after every job. If either calls "Release," the other obeys without question. Bram adds one more: "You stop when I say stop. Both of you." They agree. Trust is a tool. They use it.

**Closing Image:** Jhace writes the pact in the ledger's front page. Tiffani signs it. Bram signs as witness. Three people, one broken world, one fragile agreement: *We hold what we can. We stop before we break.*

**Emotional Beat:** Mutual protection; love disguised as logistics.

### **CHAPTER 10: The Third Note — Tiffani POV**
**~1,200 words | Day 6 Post-Piercing**

**Opening:** Tiffani hears it first: a low hum threading through the city, a third frequency under the grid's dual-tone hum. It vibrates in her teeth, makes her geometry sight *waver* like heat distortion.

**Jhace Feels It:** He's in the Underface when it hits. The copper taste floods back, stronger. His bones ache. The vein *pulses* in time with the note. He runs topside, finds Tiffani at a proof-wall, hand pressed to glass, eyes wide. "You hear it?" she asks. "I feel it," he says. They trace it together.

**The Source:** It's coming from the Piercing site—the Glass Quarter workfloor where the line pierced the sky. The wreckage still vibrates with residual energy. They descend into the ruins, following the resonance. At the center: a crack in a proof-wall, glowing faintly, *singing* the third note into the world.

**The Attempt:** They try to repair it. Tiffani maps geometry; Jhace pulls appetite. But the third note *resists*. Every time he pulls heat, the crack pushes back. His vein spikes (0.8 → 1.5). Her hands lock (60% → 75%). The crack doesn't close. The note doesn't stop.

**The Realization:** Tiffani says it first: "It's not a wound. It's a scar." Jhace understands: the Piercing didn't just break Te'Oga—it *changed* it. The world will never return to what it was. The third note is permanent. The tilt is permanent. The broken grid is permanent. They can't *fix* Te'Oga. They can only *maintain* it. Forever.

**Closing Image:** They sit in the ruins, exhausted, marked hands resting between them. The third note hums through their bones. Jhace says, "We hold it. Or we lose it." Tiffani nods. "Then we hold it." Neither says the rest: *Until we can't.*

**Emotional Beat:** Acceptance of permanent struggle; partnership as survival strategy.

## **ACT IV: PARTNERSHIP**

### **CHAPTER 11: Boundaries — Jhace POV**
**~1,200 words | Day 7 Post-Piercing**

**The Overreach:** Halfway through, a secondary span starts failing—unplanned, urgent. Jhace moves to it without thinking. His vein is already at 1.2; this will push it past 1.5 (their agreed limit). He doesn't care. If it falls, people die. He *pulls*.

**The Break:** Heat floods too fast. His vision narrows to a tunnel. Nausea hits like a fist. The copper taste becomes iron. His hand locks on the beam, tremoring violently. He can't let go. The world tilts. He hears Tiffani's voice from far away: "Jhace. Release." He can't. She says it again, louder: "RELEASE." Her hand covers his, pulls it off the beam. He staggers, hits the ground, vomits.

**The Aftermath:** Vein spiked to 1.8. Bram checks him with clinical anger: "You broke the pact." Jhace's defense is weak: "People would have died." Bram's counter is brutal: "And when *you* die from pushing too hard, who holds the next span?" Silence. Jhace has no answer.

**The Debrief:** Tiffani doesn't yell. She sits beside him and says, quiet: "I can't do this if you won't trust me to call stop." Her hands are shaking—not from SoulPulse, from fear. He realizes: she's terrified of losing him. Not because they're partners. Because they're *partners*. He nods. "I trust you." She exhales. "Good. Don't make me prove it again."

**Closing Image:** They adjust the pact. New rule: if either one overreaches, the other has veto power for next three jobs. No argument. No exception. Trust enforced by structure.

**Emotional Beat:** Vulnerability as strength; love emerging through boundaries.

### **CHAPTER 12: The Pact — Tiffani POV**
**~1,200 words | Night, Day 7 Post-Piercing**

**Emotional Beat:** Independence claimed; purpose defined; partnership named.

### **CHAPTER 15: What Holds — Jhace POV**
**~1,200 words | Day 10 Post-Piercing**

**Bram's Question:** He joins them, asks the question everyone's been asking: "What now?" Jhace answers: "We hold. As long as we can." Bram nods. "And when you can't?" Tiffani answers: "Someone else holds." It's not a happy answer. It's the only honest one.

**The Return:** They return to the Underface. The grid hums its wrong song. Jhace places his marked hand on a riser. Tiffani maps geometry with her sight. They find a saddle pin shy a quarter-turn—the same problem he corrected a week ago, before the world broke. He corrects it again. Marks it with chalk. X-in-circle. Done and checked.

**Closing Image:** They walk the Underface together, checking risers, marking clamps, listening and seeing. The work is the same. The world is not. But the work still matters. They still matter. As long as they hold, the city holds. As long as the city holds, there's a reason to wake up tomorrow.

Jhace pauses at Junction 2-nine, hand on iron, listening. The third note hums below. He doesn't fear it anymore. He knows it now. It's the sound of the broken world. And he's learned to work in harmony with it.

Tiffani's hand covers his. "Wholeness," she says—their private word for what they make together. He nods. "Wholeness."

They return to the work. Not fixing. *Maintaining*. Together.

**Final Line:** *"The world broke. We learned to hold it. That was enough."*

**Emotional Beat:** Acceptance, commitment, love defined by action rather than words.

# INTERLUDE PLACEMENT LOGIC

**Document Version**: 1.0  
**Created**: October 31, 2025  
**Purpose**: Define interlude placement rules and build-up/break-down methodology  
**Usage**: Reference for Perplexity Space when determining interlude positioning in novella bibles  

---

## INTERLUDE OVERVIEW

**Interludes** are god-vessel POV chapters (800 words each) that provide glimpses into faction activities. Each novella contains **2 interludes** featuring **2 different god-vessels**.

**Purpose**:
- Show god-vessel perspective (doctrine, followers, operations)
- Provide horror/surveillance contrast to Jhace's heroic struggle
- Build world beyond Jhace's immediate awareness
- Create unpredictability (readers cannot predict when interludes occur)

---

## PLACEMENT VARIATION

Interludes vary placement across novellas to prevent predictability:

**N1**: After Ch.3, After Ch.11  
**N2**: After Ch.4, After Ch.12  
**N3**: After Ch.5, After Ch.10  
**N4**: After Ch.2, After Ch.14  
**N5**: After Ch.11 (only one, Auditor Epilogue absorbs second)  

**Pattern**: Early/mid, mid/late, early/late, very early/very late, late only

**Principle**: Never place interludes at same chapter positions across consecutive novellas.

---

## BUILD-UP VS. BREAK-DOWN

Each interlude uses either **build-up** (pre-crisis setup) or **break-down** (post-crisis consequences), never both in same novella.

### Build-Up Methodology
**Definition**: A-plot events before interlude setup what interlude reveals.

**Example**:
- **A-plot (Ch.4)**: Jhace notices dome scars, geometric patterns appearing

## 1. VEIN SUBSTRATE: THE INVISIBLE STRESS ARCHITECTURE

### What Are Veins?

**Veins** are invisible channels of structural stress that exist within all materials (objects, living tissue, the planetary substrate itself).

**Pre-Piercing state:** Veins exist but are **inert**—stress channels locked in place, unresponsive to will or intention.

**Post-Piercing state:** The projectile's alien materials cause **vein-infection**—veins throughout Te'Oga become **responsive to conviction and Soulpulse manipulation**.

### Vein States: Three Conditions

Every vein exists in one of three states:

#### 1. Stable

**Definition:** Veins in equilibrium; stress distributed normally; material/body functioning as intended.

**Indicators:** No sensations; object holds; body healthy; no tremor or pain.

**Manipulation:** Stable veins are difficult to deliberately activate; require high conviction and precise application.

**Narrative use:** Characters describe stable objects as "holding" or "settled"; bodies feel "intact" or "whole".

#### 2. Stressed

**Definition:** Veins under unusual pressure; stress accumulating; material/body approaching failure point.

**Indicators:** Humming sensation; pressure-feeling (like water-weight); localized pain; tremor in proximate tissue.

**Manipulation:** Stressed veins are easiest to manipulate; they respond to conviction quickly; cost is moderate.

**Narrative use:** Characters describe stressed objects as "creaking" or "singing" (forbidden: "humming like a tuning fork"); bodies feel "stretched" or "tight".

#### 3. Rupturing

**Definition:** Veins in cascading failure; stress exceeding maximum threshold; material/body approaching structural collapse.

**Indicators:** Intense pain; visible cracks appearing; explosive sensation (pressure-release imminent); nosebleeds if in living tissue.

**Manipulation:** Rupturing veins cannot be stabilized through normal Soulpulse (cost too high; would kill user); can only be managed through Wholeness-braiding (accepting the failure and integrating around it) or accelerated through Entropy (allowing graceful collapse).

**Narrative use:** Characters describe rupturing objects as "screaming" (forbidden: "singing like a broken instrument"); bodies feel "shattering" or "dissolving from inside".

### Vein Density: Geographic Distribution

Vein density (responsive veins per cubic meter) varies by location post-Piercing:

| Location | Vein Density | Responsiveness | Cost Modifier |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Grindheim (Underface, near entry) | 95%+ | Extreme (veins respond instantly) | x1.5 cost (veins too sensitive) |
| Mid-Underface cities | 70–80% | High (veins respond reliably) | x1.0 cost (baseline) |
| Underface periphery | 40–50% | Moderate (veins respond inconsistently) | x0.8 cost (easier manipulation) |
| Curtain zone | Fluctuates wildly | Chaotic (veins respond unpredictably) | x2.0+ cost (dangerous) |
| Outer Crown entry | 60–70% | High (veins respond reliably) | x1.0 cost (baseline) |
| Mid-Outer Crown | 30–50% | Moderate (veins respond inconsistently) | x0.8 cost (easier manipulation) |
| Outer Crown periphery | 10–20% | Low (veins barely respond) | x0.5 cost (very difficult to activate) |

**Narrative consequence:** Magic is hardest (costliest) in Grindheim; easiest in periphery; fluctuates unpredictably in Curtain zone.

## 2. WHAT SOULPULSE IS (HARD DEFINITION)

### Core Mechanism

**Soulpulse** is the deliberate manipulation of vein state through focused conviction and directed internal energy.

**Process:**
1. User senses vein(s) in a material or living tissue
2. User focuses conviction on specific vein target
3. User channels internal energy (metabolic, emotional, or both) into the target vein
4. Vein responds by shifting state (stable → stressed, stressed → rupturing, or reversed)
5. Material/body responds to vein state change (object strengthens/weakens; wound heals/tears; structure adjusts)

### Prerequisites for Successful Soulpulse

**User must possess:**

1. **Vein sensitivity** — ability to sense veins within materials/bodies
   - Post-Piercing baseline: 70% of population develops some sensitivity
   - Trainable: with practice, sensitivity increases
   - Genetic component: some people naturally gifted; others require years of practice

2. **Conviction** — psychological state of absolute certainty + grounding + acceptance of cost
   - **Certainty:** User must know exactly what they intend to accomplish (vague intentions fail)
   - **Grounding:** User must be emotionally anchored (panic/fear causes manipulation to fail)
   - **Cost acceptance:** User must consciously accept the physical toll (hesitation causes incomplete manipulation or backlash)

3. **Energy reserves** — metabolic and emotional capacity to fuel the manipulation
   - Soulpulse draws on internal energy (blood sugar, emotional intensity, willpower)
   - Depletion causes fatigue, tremor, nosebleeds
   - Different principles draw on different energy types (Form = willpower; Wholeness = emotional; Purity = rage; Entropy = acceptance)

### What Soulpulse CANNOT Do

❌ **Cross significant distances** (must sense veins directly; range ~5 meters maximum with training)  
❌ **Work on dead matter** (veins become inert; no responsiveness)  
❌ **Affect non-biological material** (metals, stone respond weakly; biological substrate responds more readily)  
❌ **Reverse permanent damage** (scarred tissue has dead veins; cannot restore)  
❌ **Save someone at the moment of death** (veins rupture completely; no recovery point)

## 3. ACCESS REQUIREMENT: CONVICTION

### What Is Conviction?

**Conviction** is the triple-state psychological condition required to activate Soulpulse:

#### Element 1: Certainty

**Definition:** Absolute clarity about intended outcome; no ambiguity or hesitation.

**Examples of sufficient certainty:**
- "I will stabilize this child's fractured ribs" (specific, measurable)
- "I will heal the poison in this person's blood" (specific target, clear method)
- "I will strengthen this scaffold until it holds for one hour" (time-bound, specific)

**Examples of insufficient certainty:**
- "I hope I can fix this" (hope is not certainty)
- "Maybe I can stop the bleeding" (doubt present)
- "I'll try to make it better" (vague intention)

**Consequence of insufficient certainty:** Manipulation fails; veins do not respond; no cost incurred but no benefit either.

#### Element 2: Grounding

**Definition:** Emotional stability and presence; ability to remain focused despite pain, fear, or external crisis.

**Grounding sources:**
- Love/attachment (protection of someone you love grounds you in purpose)
- Competence confidence (mastery in your craft grounds you in capability)
- Physical anchoring (feet on ground, breath controlled, body present)

The Underface greeted morning like a tired god pretending not to limp. Heat rose through old iron in polite pulses, a throat-clearing that said, I’m still here, don’t make me prove it. Jhace walked it the way some people walk back roads to remember who they hate. Chalk behind his ear. Hands that knew where stone kept its secrets. Bram followed with the kind of patience that only grows in men who cannot be moved and will not be moved, which is not quite the same thing.

They didn’t speak at first. Talking before the grid had chosen its key was like arguing inside a hymn. The pipes breathed. Braces remembered their vows. Somewhere a drip counted to itself and lost the number around forty. Jhace put his palm to a riser and listened with skin. The metal told him a story in one syllable: almost. Almost true. Almost faithful. Almost not your business until it is.

“Frame,” Bram said, because ritual, because men need a smaller god before breakfast. Jhace touched here and here and here, anointing bolts with attention the way some bless foreheads with oil. He tightened nothing, loosened nothing, made no sound; the act of listening is a tool you don’t lend out. The hum came easier after, which pleased nobody and kept everybody alive.

A banner drooped in the vent-draft, its dye insisting on precision with the grin of a loan shark. Corlexi Engineering Solutions—Precision First. The corner had curled into a question mark, which felt honest. Slogans wore out faster than clamps. If you said Precision First enough, you could forget the part where budgets come second and gravity never pays its tab.

They passed the bathhouse feed. Steam rose in prayers that left stains. Two women carried wooden baskets and an argument. “Salee-blessed healer took the cough right out of him,” one said, washing the sentence with faith. “Salee took your coin,” the other said, washing her hands with skepticism. The grid collected their words and hummed on, unpersuaded. If belief could hold weight, nobody would fall. Belief can’t. Ask a floor.

The city did not understand itself. Cities are crowds that fake a single posture. Jhace cared for the posture because he remembered what happens when it forgets to stand—air turning to powder in the lungs, the small sounds people make when the ceiling becomes a sky with teeth. He had enough ghosts. He kept count even when he pretended not to.

At Junction Two-nine, the grid sulked. The way a dog sulks when it knows the rule and wants to try the rule on for weakness. Jhace placed his hand where iron throbs. The note under the note said, I would like to fail in a dignified manner; may I. He said no with his wrist, the simplest liturgy in the craft. The pitch edged toward honest. He marked an X-in-circle. Chalk is a poor substitute for gratitude and what we have.

“You hear it different,” Bram said. Not a question.

“It says almost. I’m telling it not to,” Jhace said.

“That all?”

“That’s enough.”

They chewed bread on a brace that hadn’t earned it. Bram told the glass bird story—his niece’s miracle with wings too delicate for a world with elbows. “I keep it in a box,” he said.

“It’s already in the wrong place,” Jhace said. “Boxes are smaller wrong places.”

“Stew at Hana’s,” Bram tried. “Loud children. You can pretend they’re machinery.”

“Children aren’t machinery,” Jhace said. “Machinery is fixable.”

“We’ll see,” which meant no and maybe at once. Bram did not press. He had learned the shape of Jhace’s refusals and stopped trying to hammer them into doors.

They entered the narrow where stone pinched the corridor into single-file thinking. On the wall, a proclamation ghosted by time: MOBEL INSPECTIONS—SAFETY PURIFIES. Below, in a neat hand: let what must fall, fall. Correct, perhaps. Useful, less so. Jhace touched the letters like cold water and moved on.

Here is what it felt like to be Jhace: the world was a beast suspecting a leash, and his hand was a word it remembered from when it was less wild. He could sort tired from treacherous with his thumb. He knew where someone had painted hope on a crack and called it a fix. Honest breaks were simple. Dishonest holds killed families and wrote history as a shrug.

Toward Striking, the music changed. Not volume, not tone—angle. If sound can slant, it did. The grid kept its public voice while rehearsing a private stunt. You wouldn’t notice unless your bones had trained for years under the discipline of listening for the wrong kind of silence. Jhace had. He stopped beside iron that didn’t need stopping. He pressed his palm until flesh remembered it was an instrument.

The hum parted and revealed a higher thing. Thin as filament, clean as a corrected figure, superior the way tutors are superior when their hands are soft. Direction slid off it, ashamed. The note did not ask to be placed. It permitted being noticed and waited to see who would embarrass himself first by naming it.

“What,” Bram said, and only that.

“Wrong key,” Jhace said. “Not ours.”

“Topside?”

“Topside doesn’t make this kind of mistake.”

Jhace learned the outline of a new absence. The higher thing wasn’t in the metal; it was past it, like a story a child tells while trying out the grammar of lies. Press harder and it slid. Let go and it returned in its shoes.

He did the small work because small work keeps you from becoming the man who points at horizons. Bolts. Collars. Check again. Put your ear near the skin of the world and ask it for one true thing. Accept the answer might be No. You are not owed prophecy, only labor.

Market air met them on the stair: spice, glass dust, the chalk of traders who believe in numbers until dusk, then believe in shouting. A proof-wall caught afternoon and pretended to be water. Through it, the east looked like an honest line. Lines lie. Jhace’s eyes took the gift. The rest of him filed a complaint.

An announcement board choked through a sentence about inspections—remember: the Crown values your safety—and quit. A hawker lifted an angle-cage and swore it could measure regret. A child dragged a toy cart with a wheel that kept trying to defect. The sound wrote a theme on the day and signed it badly.

The banner moved again: Precision First, its torn corner tapping stone like a man who has already decided you won’t open the door. Jhace almost knotted it secure. He didn’t. Letting it fall felt like telling the truth in a language nobody thanks you for.

“Stew,” Bram said, anchoring the day with an ordinary noun.

“Tell Hana to keep it warm,” Jhace said, and earned the ghost of a smile. Lie gently if you must; it stains slower.

He put his hand on one last riser because prayer is what you do with your best habit when no one is watching. The old hum came up faithful. Under it, the thin note returned, smug and bloodless, an arrow you notice only after it has passed through what you meant to protect. It wasn’t louder. It was simply more itself.

He didn’t bless it with a name. Names are levers and levers oblige you to lift. Hands in pockets, truant saint, he nodded to Bram and walked into a city that mistook standing for surviving. The grid sang on in the key of almost. The higher thing tuned its knife where no one could point.
