Short answer: there is no “safe” $10 bet. If you must bet, the lowest-risk move is a big moneyline favorite, and you’re trading upside for that lower risk.

Here’s how I’d approach it right now.

---

### 1. What “safest” actually means

For a single $10 bet on BetMGM, “safest” = highest probability of winning, which usually means:

- Simple **moneyline** (just “who wins”)  
- On a **heavy favorite** (big negative odds, like -300, -400, etc.)  
- No parlays, no “same game specials,” no crazy props

The trade-off: you win a **small** amount if it hits and lose the full $10 if it doesn’t.

Example from today’s board at major books:

- **NBA: Timberwolves ML vs Kings**  
  Approx line at other books: **around -400** (Timberwolves heavy favorites). citeturn1search22  

What that means:

- Implied win prob ≈ 80%  
- Bet $10 → profit ≈ $2.50 if they win, lose $10 if they don’t  

BetMGM’s exact line will be close but not identical; check the NBA moneylines in the app.

---

### 2. How to make a low-risk $10 bet on BetMGM

1. Open BetMGM → go to **Sportsbook → NBA** (or NHL / NFL, same logic).  
2. Look for **big favorites** on the moneyline (numbers like **-300 or worse** on the favorite).  
3. Tap that team’s **moneyline**, stake **$10**, and place a straight bet.  

Avoid for “safest”:

- Parlays / SGPs  
- Longshot props (+400, +800 type stuff)  
- Live chasing if it loses

---

### 3. If you meant “casino” instead of sportsbook

There is no “safe” $10 casino bet. House edge always wins long-term.

If you’re going to do it anyway, lower-damage options *in theory* are:

- Low-minimum **blackjack** with decent rules  
- Or **baccarat / mini-baccarat**

But online, rules can be worse and edges higher. Slots, jackpots, and roulette are all worse from a math standpoint.

---

### 4. Reality check

- The only genuinely **safe** $10 bet is not betting it.  
- If $10 is lunch money, don’t risk it.  
- If it’s truly “fun money,” then a straight moneyline on a heavy favorite (like the Timberwolves example) is about as conservative as sports betting gets, but it can still absolutely lose. citeturn1search22
