## **Outline for "Deicide"**

---

### **I. High-Level Premise**

- **Title:** *Deicide*
- **Genre:** Cosmic Horror / Metaphysical Sci-Fi / Psychological Fantasy
- **Core Theme:** The murder of a god by its creation—man—and the existential, psychological, and metaphysical consequences that follow.
- **Core Conflict:** Humanity's reckoning with divine abandonment, rebellion against metaphysical determinism, and the philosophical implications of killing God.

---

### **II. Setting**

#### A. **Multilayered Reality**
1. **The Physical Realm**
   - Earth: decaying, post-enlightenment, full of psychic dissonance and techno-religious decay.
   - Civilization: fractured, scattered in cultist states and forgotten cities.
   - Technology and mysticism are fused—e.g., quantum rituals, AI prophecies, reality-bending devices.

2. **The Astral Layer (The Reverie)**
   - A dreamlike interstitial plane where thoughts shape reality.
   - Accessed via mind-expanding rituals, sacred geometries, or quantum consciousness tech.
   - Here, remnants of god’s consciousness may linger—fractured and insane.

3. **The Divine Corpse (The God Husk)**
   - Literal remnants of the dead god’s body drifting in metaphysical space or embedded into Earth’s core.
   - Worshipped, exploited, feared. Divine rot infects reality.

---

### **III. Mythology & Lore**

#### A. **The God (The Architect)**
- Ancient, possibly eternal being who created reality.
- Bound by laws of metaphysical causality and entropy.
- Gave humanity the spark of divinity—an act of hubris or sabotage.

#### B. **The Deicide**
- Humanity discovered the method to kill its creator.
- Could involve:
  - Esoteric science (neuroquantum weaponry)
  - Collective psychic rupture
  - Cosmic inversion (belief collapse en masse)

#### C. **The Fractured Divinity**
- God did not fully die—its consciousness splintered across planes.
- Manifestations:
  - Echoes (half-conscious fragments)
  - Theophages (beings who consume godstuff)
  - The Cult of Mourning (religion based on grieving God)

#### D. **The Theocide Myth**
- Split among:
  - **Cultists:** Seek to resurrect the god or punish humanity.
  - **Liberationists:** See it as the first step to full autonomy.
  - **Mystics:** Believe the god never existed; it's a mythic trauma.

---

### **IV. Characters & Archetypes**

#### A. **The Pilgrim (Protagonist)**
- Role: Seeker of truth, possibly mentally fractured.
- Background:
  - Witnessed the Deicide.
  - Suffers divine echo syndrome (haunted by residual fragments).
  - Mission: Understand or undo what happened.

#### B. **The Heretic Prophet**
- Role: Once a religious figure; now excommunicated.
- Possesses forbidden knowledge—believes the god still lives as pain.

#### C. **The Quantum Monks**
- Order that bridges metaphysics and mathematics.
- Use symbology, equations, and rituals to stabilize or unravel reality.

#### D. **The Theophage**
- God-eater. Consumes divine fragments for power.
- Mad, powerful, grotesquely transcendent.

#### E. **The Architect’s Echo**
- Semi-sentient remnant of the divine mind.
- Acts as a trickster, prophet, and tormentor.

---

### **V. Plot Structure**

#### **Act I: Fractures of Faith**
1. World introduction: Post-god society, psychologically unwell.
2. The protagonist suffers psychic tremors from the Deicide event.
3. Catalyst: The re-emergence of divine echoes.
4. The journey begins toward the God Husk or one of the divine artifacts.

#### **Act II: Through the Reverie**
1. Exploration of the astral/dream layers.
2. Encounter with cults, theophages, and prophet factions.
3. Flashbacks or revelations of the true Deicide mechanism.
4. Internal conflict deepens—sanity degrades, god-echoes increase.

#### **Act III: Confronting the Corpse**
1. Reach the divine remains—a corrupted, non-Euclidean plane.
2. Confrontation with Architect’s Echo.
3. Final choice:
   - Attempt resurrection
   - Complete erasure
   - Transcendence: become godlike and restart existence.

- **Knowledge as Madness:** The price of truth is sanity.
