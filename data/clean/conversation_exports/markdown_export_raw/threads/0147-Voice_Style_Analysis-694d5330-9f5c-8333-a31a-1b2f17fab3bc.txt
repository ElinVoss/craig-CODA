## Planet Overview and Climate Bands

### Shape, Size, and Axial State

- Te'Oga is an Earthlike terrestrial planet (gravity and atmosphere close enough that readers don't need constant explanation)
- Before the Piercing: axial tilt ~23°, recognizable seasons
- After the Piercing: axis nearly upright (~0.8°), eliminating seasons

### Post-Piercing Climate Bands

With almost no axial tilt, Te'Oga stabilizes into permanent latitudinal bands:

| Zone | Condition | Characteristics |
|------|-----------|------------------|
| **Equatorial Belt (Heat Reach)** | Perpetual high sun, hot and humid | Dense jungles, swamps, heavy river systems; limited large cities due to heat and disease |
| **Mid-Latitude Bands (Temper Grids)** | Eternal "late spring/early autumn" | Mild, stable, ideal for agriculture and dense urbanization; Crown sits here |
| **Polar Zones (Cold Expanses)** | Perpetual cold, long ice reaches | Sparse population; mining outposts, monasteries, exile colonies |

Characters feel the climate as a constant: no seasons, no "summer" or "winter" language—only regional heat, temper, or cold.

## JHACE & TIFFANI: THE CORE PARTNERSHIP

**Structure:** 12 chapters + 2 interludes spanning 4 distinct phases

### Phase 1: Mutual Judgment (Ch 1-3)

**Duration:** Tide 0 (Pre-Piercing through Piercing)

**Jhace's View:** Tiffani is the perfectionist who prevents work. Guild gossip paints her as someone who refuses commissions if specifications aren't perfect, causing projects to stall. He assumes that sort of person will get good workers killed by refusing to act.

**Tiffani's View:** Jhace is the overextending hero who thinks only he can save people. She believes he takes on too much out of ego. From a distance, it looks like savior complex disguised as quality control.

**Reality:** Both are wrong. They haven't met yet and are projecting their own fears onto each other based on secondhand information.

**Ch 1-2 Status:** Mutual misconceptions, no direct contact, separate observations of the anomaly  
**Ch 3 Status:** First sight under crisis. Tiffani sees Jhace holding a collapsing building. Her first reaction isn't awe but analytical curiosity: "What did you do?" matters more than "how brave."

## CORE ARC

N01 tells the story of how Jhace Torrins discovers his Soulpulse ability during the Piercing, how he and Tiffani Merrow learn to trust one another, and how they decide to face the city's crisis together. They navigate guild politics, institutional power plays, and the physical cost of magic. By the end of N01, they have formed a partnership in both work and love, though it remains physically unconsummated due to exhaustion and circumstances.

---

## WORLD STATE AT START

### Pre-Piercing Baseline
Saint Merro is a terraced city with a functional guild system. Higher districts are wealthier and more stable; lower districts are older and fragile. Technology is roughly renaissance/proto-industrial: water and wind mills, simple steam engines, printing presses, advanced metallurgy. No electricity. Light sources: oil and candles. Communication: runners and signal flags. No one has ever seen or done anything like Soulpulse.

### The Piercing Event (Tide 0)
A massive cylindrical object enters the atmosphere at impossible angle and speeds, warping light as it passes. It shears across the sky without touching ground and continues into space. Shockwaves shatter structures. Dust and metallic particles rain down. Many buildings collapse; hundreds die. Traditional physics fails at quantum level. This is the inciting incident of the entire saga.

### Post-Piercing Conditions
The city is partially destroyed. Institutions are overwhelmed. Resources are scarce. People are traumatized. New abilities (Soulpulse) begin to emerge in roughly 1 in 500 individuals. The heat-grid, critical to winter survival, is failing. Guilds fracture over how to respond. The Regulatory and Reconstruction Office (RRO) forms to coordinate relief but quickly drifts toward control and bureaucracy.

## PRIMARY CHARACTERS

### Jhace Torrins
**Age:** 29 Turns  
**Profession:** Structural engineer, heat-grid inspector  

**Backstory:** His parents died in a building collapse when he was 7. Ever since, he obsessed over preventing preventable deaths. Works alone to avoid sharing his anxiety with others. Refuses apprentices to keep his hypervigilance from infecting someone else.

**Soulpulse:** Instinctively activates during Piercing. Discovers it requires direct contact and protective desperation. Learns cost through repeated use: tremor, pain, exhaustion. Stage progression moves from baseline to Stage 1 through this novella. Reaches the threshold of Stage 2 by final chapters.

**Psychology:** Hypervigilant, guilt-driven, physically oriented. Terse, literal, thinks in terms of weight and vibration. Hates being called a hero. Lies to others and himself that he's fine. Needs to prevent death above all.

**Voice:** Short terse sentences. Physical-sensory language. Deflection through blunt statements. Guilt-driven humor lands accidentally; genuine laughter is rare and loud.

**N01 Arc:** Moves from isolated vigilante to co-lead in a partnership. Learns to trust Tiffani's analytical mind. Accepts help. Admits romantic feelings. Commits to working together when the world is broken.

Voice Capture: What You Just Did (Sample 1 Analysis)
Opening Rhythm: Body-First, Then Thought
"It just wouldn't stop. This tremor."

Fragment as anchor. You start with the physical fact, then name it.

Not "His hand was shaking" (distant narrator). You drop us into the sensation first, then explain.

Thought Tagging Style
"This is Bullshit, Jhace thought"

You use direct internal dialogue with attribution tags, not italics or deep-POV immersion.

Casual profanity in thought = natural for you. It's not performance; it's how the brain talks to itself.

Environmental Setup: Sparse, Functional
"Furnaces lined the walls here. None were fired. Though it wasn't cold."

You don't linger on description. You give just enough to place the body in space.

The contradiction (not cold, but he could use layers) = physical discomfort as emotional texture, not weather report.

Emotional Deflection Through Practicality
"Tears are expensive. He couldn't afford tears."

This is your signature move in reflective mode: turning emotion into transactional language.

"Expensive" reframes grief as a resource problem. That's how someone who's done time thinks—everything costs something, and you budget accordingly.

Rage as Punctuation
"Why now!!?!?" He screamed to the unlit forge. "You could have given me this when i needed it!"

Outburst breaks the stillness. You don't build to it with paragraphs of internal monologue—you just erupt.

Screaming at an object (the forge) instead of a person = isolation + helplessness. He's arguing with the universe because there's no one else to blame.

Repetition as Grief Loop
"i could have saved them, then. / i could have saved them. He thought once more. / Then."

Lowercase "i" might be typo or stylistic—but the repetition is deliberate.

The loop tightens: full sentence → fragment → single word.

That final "Then." = the moment that didn't happen. The word hangs there, unfinished, because the救 never came.

What This Teaches Me About Your Prose DNA
1. Grief = Logistics Failure
You don't write sadness as abstract emotion. You write it as cost accounting.

"Tears are expensive."

"I should have been able to help."

The tremor is a bill he's paying after the crisis, when it's useless.

This is someone who's spent years in systems where everything—commissary, phone time, safety—has a price. Emotion gets the same treatment.

2. Rage Breaks Syntax
When the character loses control, you lose control of sentence structure.

Questions get multiple punctuation marks.

Capitals for emphasis.

Fragments get shorter until they're just single words.

You don't describe a breakdown. You perform it in the prose itself.

3. Stillness Isn't Calm
Even in a quiet scene (no combat, no crisis), your prose paces.

"This tremor."

"Not today."

"Then."

The fragments aren't rest stops—they're held breath. Stillness in your writing is tension, not peace.

# Lexicon & Style Guide

**Prose Rules and Measurement Usage for Confluence Chronicles**

---

## Document Purpose

This guide defines how the story is written on the page: narrative voice, formatting, language constraints, and how Te'Oga measurement systems are used in prose. It keeps all 30 novellas consistent in tone, clarity, and in-world terminology.

---

## Prose Laws (Non-Negotiable)

### Point of View

- Camera stays inside POV character's sensory and conceptual limits—no omniscient commentary in main chapters

### Tense

- Default: **tight past tense** for main chapters
- Short present-tense flashes allowed only for immediate, high-stress sensations (pain, impact, realization), and must be brief

### Language Constraints

- **No metaphors or similes** in main chapters
  - Do not write "like" / "as if" comparisons for imagery
  - Describe what is literally present: light, sound, motion, weight, temperature, stress
- Descriptions must be **plain, concrete, and physical**
  - "The beam bowed two fingers under the load" is correct
  - "The beam sagged like an old man" is not

### Magic Description (Grounded and Anime-Escalated)

- Soulpulse use always described through **material effects and bodily cost**, not abstract energy:
  - Show beams straightening, glass knitting, joints locking, stone crumbling
  - Show shaking hands, shortness of breath, pain, exhaustion
- Even at late-saga anime levels:
  - No color-coded auras ("blue aura of Purity," etc.)
  - No vague "energy blasts" without specifying pressure, shrapnel, heat, concussion, or structural result
  - If someone "flies," ground it (platforms of locked debris, wind pressure control, etc.)

### Cost Signaling

Every on-page use of Soulpulse must show **some** cost, scaled to intensity:

- Light use: tremor, tension, extra breathing
- Heavy use: shaking limbs, partial collapse, muddled thoughts
- Fathom Break / god-tier: near-total collapse, lasting injury

There is no cost-free use of Soulpulse. If it looks effortless, the pain is deferred, not erased.

---

## Measurement Lexicon Usage

All in-world measurement uses Te'Oga systems: time, distance, and mass. Metric/imperial terms **never** appear in character-facing prose.

### Time Units

Canonical units:

- **Tide** – day (~24 hours)
- **Anneal** – 5 Tides (week)
- **Crucible** – 7 Anneals (35 Tides; month)
- **Casting** – 9 Crucibles (year)

Daily periods within a Tide:

- **Kindling** – dawn / start of work
- **Pouring** – mid-morning
- **Shaping** – midday
- **Striking** – afternoon
- **The Quench** – dusk / rest

**Usage rules:**
- Use Te'Oga terms directly:
  - "Two Tides later," not "two days later"
  - "Within this Anneal," not "within this week"
  - "In his thirtieth Casting," not "at thirty years old"
- One-time soft equivalences ("a Tide—the length of a day") may be used sparingly in early N01 to orient new readers; avoid afterward

### Distance Units

From smallest to largest:

- **Finger** (~2 cm)
- **Hand** (~10 cm)
- **Span** (~23 cm)
- **Forearm** (~45 cm)
- **Stride** (~90 cm)
- **Bar** (~1.8 m)
- **Rod** (~9 m)
- **Cable** (~45 m)
- **Vein-span** (~225 m)
- **Circuit** (~1.1 km)
- **Grid** (~5.5 km)
- **Reach** (~27.5 km)
- **Expanse** (~137 km)

**Usage rules:**
- Close detail: finger, hand, span ("The gap was three fingers wide")
- Room / workshop: stride, bar ("The arch stood a bar and a half tall")
- Street / block / infrastructure: rod, cable, vein-span
- Neighborhood / district / city: circuit, grid
- Regional / continental: reach, expanse

Avoid metric/imperial in narrative. If you need a "translation," keep it in out-of-story material (bibles, notes), not in the prose.

### Mass Units

Key units:

- **Grain** – smallest particles
- **Pinch** – powders, spices, frit
- **Handful** – small solids
- **Stone** – personal lifting weight (~7.5 kg)
- **Load** – cart burden
- **Ingot** – major structural element mass
- **Pour** – foundry batch

def extract_section(text, header):
    # extract markdown section with header starting with '##' or '###' etc
    pattern=re.compile(rf"(^#+\s*{re.escape(header)}\s*$)", re.MULTILINE)
    m=pattern.search(text)
    if not m: 
        return None
    start=m.start()
    # end at next same or higher level header
    level=len(m.group(1).split()[0])
    # find next header of level<= current
    end=len(text)
    for m2 in re.finditer(r"^#{1,%d}\s+.*$"%level, text[m.end():], re.MULTILINE):
        end=m.end()+m2.start()
        break
    return text[start:end].strip()

import React, { useState, useMemo, useEffect } from "react";

// ...

const copyToClipboard = async (text, id) => {
  try {
    await navigator.clipboard.writeText(text);
  } catch (err) {
    // Fallback for environments where Clipboard API is blocked
    const ta = document.createElement("textarea");
    ta.value = text;
    ta.style.position = "fixed";
    ta.style.left = "-9999px";
    ta.style.top = "-9999px";
    document.body.appendChild(ta);
    ta.focus();
    ta.select();
    document.execCommand("copy");
    document.body.removeChild(ta);
  }

  setCopiedBeat(id);
  setTimeout(() => setCopiedBeat(null), 2000);
};
