# Tide
- **Core meaning (n.)**: The **periodic rise and fall of sea level** caused primarily by the Moon’s gravity, modulated by the Sun, with Earth’s rotation producing typically **two highs and two lows** each day.  
  - **Spring tide**: larger range at **new/full moon**.  
  - **Neap tide**: smaller range at **first/third quarter**.
- **Figurative (n.)**: A **broad, impersonal force or trend** that carries things along (“the tide of war,” “the tide of opinion”).
- **Verb**: To drift or move with the tide.

**Writer’s note**: “Tide” implies **cyclical pull** and **inevitability**, not random waves or currents.

# Anneal
- **Core meaning (v., materials)**: To **heat** a metal or glass to a specified temperature and **cool it slowly** to **reduce internal stress**, **increase ductility**, and **decrease hardness/brittleness**.
- **Other technical uses**:
  - **DNA annealing**: Complementary strands **bind** as temperature falls.
  - **Simulated annealing (algorithms)**: A search/optimization method that mimics heating then slow cooling to escape local optima.
- **Figurative**: To **stabilize or toughen** something (a group, a resolve) through **controlled stress and recovery**, not shock.
