## 1. PRE-PIERCING WORLD STATE

### The Bore: Sealed Cylindrical Void

**Physical description (pre-Piercing):**
- Cylindrical wound through planet (entry/exit at hemispheric boundaries)
- **Diameter:** ~30 kilometers (consistent, visible from space)
- **Depth:** Full planetary penetration (passes completely through Te'Oga from entry to exit)
- **State:** SEALED — impenetrable barrier; no passage through; void maintains stability through unknown mechanism

**Why sealed:**
- Innocent-frame belief: Ancient seal from God War (the Severing); magic or craft-law locked it
- Guilty-frame reality: Deliberately sealed to contain vein-infection until triggering event (Tiffani's orchestration)

**Pre-Piercing consequences of sealed Bore:**
- Bifurcated planet: Underface and Outer Crown isolated
- Curtain zone unstable (pressure differential trying to equalize)
- Heat grid functions through indirect routing (not through Bore; around it)
- No direct atmospheric transfer between hemispheres

---

### Pre-Piercing Gravity

**Asymmetry due to Bore:**
- Gravity shifted ~0.3% toward Outer Crown (entry-hemisphere)
- Not enough to cause physical problems; compass/navigation oddities
- Inhabitants notice "heaviness" at equator (transitional zone)
- Water always flows toward Outer Crown (slight bias)

---

## 2. THE PIERCING: EVENT MECHANICS

### The Projectile

**Physical description:**
- **Shape:** Straight cylinder; same diameter as the Bore (~30km)
- **Composition:** Glass, iron, pewter, tin, sand, alien sediments (see §6)
- **Entry point:** Through entry-hemisphere atmosphere
- **Exit point:** Completely through planet; exits exit-hemisphere atmosphere
- **Velocity:** Impossible speed (faster than sound; possibly near orbital velocity)
- **Sound:** Mechanical shrieking, glass-shattering resonance (describable sound; not magical)

### The Event Sequence (N1 Ch.3)

**Seconds 0–5 (First awareness):**
- Sky cracks open above Grindheim and Shatterholm
- Projectile visible as geometric impossibility moving through sky
- Initial shock: citizens witness phenomenon that violates craft-law
- Sound arrives ~5 seconds after first sighting (delayed transmission)

**Seconds 5–30 (Main passage):**
- Projectile passes completely through Te'Oga
- Duration of visible passage: ~20–30 seconds per location
- Sky ruptures and reforms; atmospheric displacement visible
- Pressure-wave radiates outward; buildings sway; objects fall

**Seconds 30–90 (Aftermath phase):**
- Projectile exits opposite hemisphere (seen from Outer Crown)
- Atmospheric rupture begins closing (vacuum-collapse effect)
- Pressure-waves propagate globally; vein-shatter waves begin
- Sound echoes through Bore; multiple arrivals of shock-waves
- Citizens experience confusion and panic; communication chaos

**Minutes 1–60 (Consequence cascade):**
- Vein-shatter followshocks begin (rupture-grade 7.5–8.0; multiple waves)
- Atmospheric turbulence becomes standard in entry/exit zones
- Heat grid destabilizes (rupture disrupts resonance networks)
- Refugees flee toward stable zones; evacuation corridors activate

---

### What The Piercing Does (Immediate Effects)

1. **Severs the Bore completely** — Seal ruptures; straight gaping hole opens through world; no more impenetrable barrier

2. **Leaves physical scar** — Entry and exit wounds remain open permanently; atmospheric continuity established

3. **Creates atmospheric rupture** — Pressure differential at entry/exit zones causes persistent storm-like conditions

4. **Injects alien materials** — Projectile fragments embed in rupture zone; unknown elements contaminate local area

5. **Activates vein-infection** — Alien materials trigger global vein-response; Soulpulse becomes possible worldwide

6. **Disrupts heat grid** — Resonance networks fractured; heat distribution destabilized; heat-grid repair becomes N1+ priority

7. **Generates vein-shatter catastrophe** — Rupture-grade 7.5–8.0 rupture-waves propagate globally; infrastructure damage zones

# INTERLUDE PLACEMENT LOGIC

**Document Version**: 1.0  
**Created**: October 31, 2025  
**Purpose**: Define interlude placement rules and build-up/break-down methodology  
**Usage**: Reference for Perplexity Space when determining interlude positioning in novella bibles  

---

## INTERLUDE OVERVIEW

**Interludes** are god-vessel POV chapters (800 words each) that provide glimpses into faction activities. Each novella contains **2 interludes** featuring **2 different god-vessels**.

**Purpose**:
- Show god-vessel perspective (doctrine, followers, operations)
- Provide horror/surveillance contrast to Jhace's heroic struggle
- Build world beyond Jhace's immediate awareness
- Create unpredictability (readers cannot predict when interludes occur)

---

## PLACEMENT VARIATION

Interludes vary placement across novellas to prevent predictability:

**N1**: After Ch.3, After Ch.11  
**N2**: After Ch.4, After Ch.12  
**N3**: After Ch.5, After Ch.10  
**N4**: After Ch.2, After Ch.14  
**N5**: After Ch.11 (only one, Auditor Epilogue absorbs second)  

**Pattern**: Early/mid, mid/late, early/late, very early/very late, late only

**Principle**: Never place interludes at same chapter positions across consecutive novellas.

---

## BUILD-UP VS. BREAK-DOWN

Each interlude uses either **build-up** (pre-crisis setup) or **break-down** (post-crisis consequences), never both in same novella.

### Build-Up Methodology
**Definition**: A-plot events before interlude setup what interlude reveals.

**Example**:
- **A-plot (Ch.4)**: Jhace notices dome scars, geometric patterns appearing
- **Interlude 1A (after Ch.4)**: Xilcore's perspective — obsessive geometric "corrections"
- **Effect**: Reader understands what Jhace witnessed

**Usage**: When god-vessel activity is mysterious, interlude explains

---

### Break-Down Methodology
**Definition**: Interlude explains what A-plot characters witness after.

**Example**:
- **Interlude 2A (after Ch.11)**: Blemo's purge operations shown
- **A-plot (Ch.12)**: Refugees (Halrick) describe fires and purges
- **Effect**: Reader knows truth before A-plot characters do

**Usage**: When god-vessel activity has consequences, interlude provides context

---

### The Crafted Calendar System

Time flows like molten glass through the sacred mathematics of craft—five tides to shape, seven anneals to perfect, nine crucibles to complete.

---

## **Tide** (Day)

A single rotation of the world, named for the flowing rhythm of molten glass and the inexorable passage of heat through the forge.

**Phrasing:** "Three tides hence" = three days from now

**Daily divisions:**
- **Kindling** (Dawn) — First Tide; call to work; heat-grid activates to daily minimum
- **Pouring** (Mid-morning) — Second Tide; full work begins; peak energy
- **Shaping** (Noon) — Third Tide; peak heat; maximum output
- **Striking** (Afternoon) — Fourth Tide; refinement; precision work
- **Cooling Hour** (Dusk) — Fifth Tide; The Quench; work winds down; heat-grid begins daily rest

**Example usage in prose:**
- "By Kindling, the scaffold was down." (by dawn)
- "They worked through Shaping and into Striking." (through the hottest part of day)
- "The Quench brought reprieve." (evening came)

---

## **Anneal** (Week - 5 days)

The five-day cycle required for proper annealing of significant glassworks. Each day has a craft-name and thematic purpose:

| Order | Name | Meaning | Crafting Stage |
|-------|------|---------|---|
| 1 | **Heattide** | Preparation, gathering materials | Material assembly |
| 2 | **Pourtide** | Beginning work, molten state | Activation/ignition |
| 3 | **Shapetide** | Formation, the peak of creation | Maximum effort |
| 4 | **Striketide** | Refinement, hammer and tool | Precision work |
| 5 | **Cooltide** | Settling, hardening, rest | Integration/completion |

**Phrasing:** "Before the anneal cools" = within the week

**Example usage in prose:**
- "On Striketide, Jhace realized the crack ran deeper." (fourth day of week; refinement work day)
- "They had until Cooltide to finish." (by end of week)
- "Meet me at Pourtide." (second day of week; transition to active work)

---

## **Crucible** (Month - 7 anneals / 35 tides)

Seven anneals, representing the seven stages of a master crucible's use before it must be relined or replaced. The seven anneals within a Crucible are named for the crucible's lifecycle:

| Order | Name | Metaphor | Purpose |
|-------|------|----------|---------|
| 1 | **First Heat** | Foundation setting | Organizational/planning month |
| 2 | **Rising Glow** | Momentum building | Active work month |
| 3 | **Full Blaze** | Peak intensity | Crisis/climax month |
| 4 | **Steady Burn** | Sustainable rhythm | Recovery/stabilization month |
| 5 | **Ember Anneal** | Maintained heat | Consolidation month |
| 6 | **Fading Coals** | Waning intensity | Transition month |
| 7 | **Final Temper** | Hardening/locking | Resolution month |

**Phrasing:** "A crucible of mourning" = a month of grief

**Example usage in prose:**
- "In the Third Anneal, the heat peaked." (mid-month crisis)
- "By Final Temper, she had accepted it." (end of month; acceptance)

## 1. VEIN SUBSTRATE: THE INVISIBLE STRESS ARCHITECTURE

### What Are Veins?

**Veins** are invisible channels of structural stress that exist within all materials (objects, living tissue, the planetary substrate itself).

**Pre-Piercing state:** Veins exist but are **inert**—stress channels locked in place, unresponsive to will or intention.

**Post-Piercing state:** The projectile's alien materials cause **vein-infection**—veins throughout Te'Oga become **responsive to conviction and Soulpulse manipulation**.

### Vein States: Three Conditions

Every vein exists in one of three states:

#### 1. Stable

**Definition:** Veins in equilibrium; stress distributed normally; material/body functioning as intended.

**Indicators:** No sensations; object holds; body healthy; no tremor or pain.

**Manipulation:** Stable veins are difficult to deliberately activate; require high conviction and precise application.

**Narrative use:** Characters describe stable objects as "holding" or "settled"; bodies feel "intact" or "whole".

#### 2. Stressed

**Definition:** Veins under unusual pressure; stress accumulating; material/body approaching failure point.

**Indicators:** Humming sensation; pressure-feeling (like water-weight); localized pain; tremor in proximate tissue.

**Manipulation:** Stressed veins are easiest to manipulate; they respond to conviction quickly; cost is moderate.

**Narrative use:** Characters describe stressed objects as "creaking" or "singing" (forbidden: "humming like a tuning fork"); bodies feel "stretched" or "tight".

#### 3. Rupturing

**Definition:** Veins in cascading failure; stress exceeding maximum threshold; material/body approaching structural collapse.

**Indicators:** Intense pain; visible cracks appearing; explosive sensation (pressure-release imminent); nosebleeds if in living tissue.

**Manipulation:** Rupturing veins cannot be stabilized through normal Soulpulse (cost too high; would kill user); can only be managed through Wholeness-braiding (accepting the failure and integrating around it) or accelerated through Entropy (allowing graceful collapse).

**Narrative use:** Characters describe rupturing objects as "screaming" (forbidden: "singing like a broken instrument"); bodies feel "shattering" or "dissolving from inside".

### Vein Density: Geographic Distribution

Vein density (responsive veins per cubic meter) varies by location post-Piercing:

| Location | Vein Density | Responsiveness | Cost Modifier |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Grindheim (Underface, near entry) | 95%+ | Extreme (veins respond instantly) | x1.5 cost (veins too sensitive) |
| Mid-Underface cities | 70–80% | High (veins respond reliably) | x1.0 cost (baseline) |
| Underface periphery | 40–50% | Moderate (veins respond inconsistently) | x0.8 cost (easier manipulation) |
| Curtain zone | Fluctuates wildly | Chaotic (veins respond unpredictably) | x2.0+ cost (dangerous) |
| Outer Crown entry | 60–70% | High (veins respond reliably) | x1.0 cost (baseline) |
| Mid-Outer Crown | 30–50% | Moderate (veins respond inconsistently) | x0.8 cost (easier manipulation) |
| Outer Crown periphery | 10–20% | Low (veins barely respond) | x0.5 cost (very difficult to activate) |

**Narrative consequence:** Magic is hardest (costliest) in Grindheim; easiest in periphery; fluctuates unpredictably in Curtain zone.

## 2. WHAT SOULPULSE IS (HARD DEFINITION)

### Core Mechanism

**Soulpulse** is the deliberate manipulation of vein state through focused conviction and directed internal energy.

**Process:**
1. User senses vein(s) in a material or living tissue
2. User focuses conviction on specific vein target
3. User channels internal energy (metabolic, emotional, or both) into the target vein
4. Vein responds by shifting state (stable → stressed, stressed → rupturing, or reversed)
5. Material/body responds to vein state change (object strengthens/weakens; wound heals/tears; structure adjusts)

### Prerequisites for Successful Soulpulse

**User must possess:**

1. **Vein sensitivity** — ability to sense veins within materials/bodies
   - Post-Piercing baseline: 70% of population develops some sensitivity
   - Trainable: with practice, sensitivity increases
   - Genetic component: some people naturally gifted; others require years of practice

2. **Conviction** — psychological state of absolute certainty + grounding + acceptance of cost
   - **Certainty:** User must know exactly what they intend to accomplish (vague intentions fail)
   - **Grounding:** User must be emotionally anchored (panic/fear causes manipulation to fail)
   - **Cost acceptance:** User must consciously accept the physical toll (hesitation causes incomplete manipulation or backlash)

3. **Energy reserves** — metabolic and emotional capacity to fuel the manipulation
   - Soulpulse draws on internal energy (blood sugar, emotional intensity, willpower)
   - Depletion causes fatigue, tremor, nosebleeds
   - Different principles draw on different energy types (Form = willpower; Wholeness = emotional; Purity = rage; Entropy = acceptance)

### What Soulpulse CANNOT Do

❌ **Cross significant distances** (must sense veins directly; range ~5 meters maximum with training)  
❌ **Work on dead matter** (veins become inert; no responsiveness)  
❌ **Affect non-biological material** (metals, stone respond weakly; biological substrate responds more readily)  
❌ **Reverse permanent damage** (scarred tissue has dead veins; cannot restore)  
❌ **Save someone at the moment of death** (veins rupture completely; no recovery point)

## 3. ACCESS REQUIREMENT: CONVICTION

### What Is Conviction?

**Conviction** is the triple-state psychological condition required to activate Soulpulse:

#### Element 1: Certainty

**Definition:** Absolute clarity about intended outcome; no ambiguity or hesitation.

**Examples of sufficient certainty:**
- "I will stabilize this child's fractured ribs" (specific, measurable)
- "I will heal the poison in this person's blood" (specific target, clear method)
- "I will strengthen this scaffold until it holds for one hour" (time-bound, specific)

**Examples of insufficient certainty:**
- "I hope I can fix this" (hope is not certainty)
- "Maybe I can stop the bleeding" (doubt present)
- "I'll try to make it better" (vague intention)

**Consequence of insufficient certainty:** Manipulation fails; veins do not respond; no cost incurred but no benefit either.

#### Element 2: Grounding

**Definition:** Emotional stability and presence; ability to remain focused despite pain, fear, or external crisis.

**Grounding sources:**
- Love/attachment (protection of someone you love grounds you in purpose)
- Competence confidence (mastery in your craft grounds you in capability)
- Physical anchoring (feet on ground, breath controlled, body present)
- Community/crew (feeling part of something larger grounds you in collective purpose)

**Threats to grounding:**
- Panic (disassociates you from body/intention)
- Grief (overwhelms focus with emotion)
- Physical injury (pain pulls attention away)
- Doubt about worthiness (moral questioning of whether action is right)

**Consequence of insufficient grounding:** Manipulation fails or causes **backlash**—veins respond incorrectly; user experiences intense pain or temporary paralysis.

#### Element 3: Cost Acceptance

**Definition:** Conscious psychological acceptance of the permanent toll the manipulation will exact.

**What acceptance means:**
- Not intellectually knowing the cost, but *emotionally accepting* it
- Willingness to trade your physical health for the outcome
- Pre-commitment to living with the scar (literal tremor, chronic pain, sensory loss)

**How acceptance fails:**
- User hesitates mid-manipulation (thinking "this will hurt too much")
- User second-guesses the worthiness of outcome (thinking "am I really willing to sacrifice for this?")
- User tries to minimize cost through technique (thinking "I can do this efficiently"; magic doesn't work that way)

**Consequence of insufficient acceptance:** Manipulation fails at critical moment; partial vein activation causes **Hollowing cascade** (see §7).
