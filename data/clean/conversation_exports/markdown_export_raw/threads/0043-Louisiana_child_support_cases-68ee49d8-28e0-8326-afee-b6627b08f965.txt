- Louisiana’s child-support info is shown inside the DCFS “CAFÉ” self-service portal (the la.gov/CAFÉ single sign-on). The portal lists case numbers for programs (SNAP, FITAP, KCSP) and Child Support Enforcement. If a case number shows but you can’t open any details, it often means the system linked an identifier to your account without full public details being exposed. citeturn0search7turn0search13

# Why the portal can show *just a number* with no details
1. **System linking but no permission** — CAFÉ shows case IDs tied to your identity token, but sensitive details require worker access or court records.  
2. **Other program overlap** — the portal manages SNAP/TANF and child support in one view; the number might be for a different program or internal application number. citeturn0search13turn0search1  
3. **Staff created placeholder or data error** — a worker could have entered a case incorrectly or merged the wrong client. citeturn0search8  
4. **Sealed/closed/other parish case** — sometimes old or out-of-parish records show as an ID only.  
5. **Identity theft / fraud** — someone used your SSN or details to open a case or register; this is less common but not impossible. If that’s even a remote possibility, act fast. citeturn3search0

**Step A — Message the caseworker via CAFÉ message center (fastest paper trail)**  
Use the portal’s “Message Center” to create a record. Copy this and paste:

> Subject: Unknown Child Support Case Number Visible on My CAFÉ Account — Request for Verification and Removal  
>  
> Hello — my CAFÉ account (username: [your la.gov user id]) shows a Child Support case number **[CASE# shown]**. I have never been involved in or notified of any child support case, and there are no case details visible in my portal. Please:  
> 1. Confirm what that case number refers to and why it is linked to my account.  
> 2. Provide the case participant names, parish, and court/case file associated with this number.  
> 3. If this is an error, remove this case number from my account and confirm in writing.  
>  
> I am also attaching a screenshot of my CAFÉ view. Please respond by email to [your email] or through CAFÉ messages. Thank you,  
> [Your name, DOB (optional), last 4 of SSN (if comfortable), phone]

# If DCFS drags feet or claims confidentiality
- Ask for the **parish** and **court case number** — courts are public in most situations and you can pull the docket. If the name/case isn’t yours, take that printout to DCFS and demand correction.  
- If the case is court-based and you are being sued/ordered, **consult a family law attorney immediately** — you may need to file a response or a motion to set aside. I’m not an attorney, but you don’t want to ignore something with a court stamp.

# What to expect (without hand-holding timelines)
- You may get a quick explanation (data merge) or you might get a paper chase. DCFS will usually be able to tell you whether the ID is a cross-match or if a worker created a placeholder — but they might require identity verification. If identity theft is involved, multiple agencies (DCFS, police, FTC, credit bureaus) will be needed.
