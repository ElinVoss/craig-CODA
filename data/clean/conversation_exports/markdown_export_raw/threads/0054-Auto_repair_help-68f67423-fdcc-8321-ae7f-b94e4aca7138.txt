My friend has a 2016 Honda Odyssey, and I need to, uh, take the serpentine belt off, but the bolt that holds the tensioner pulley is stripped. Can I just use a breaker bar against the pulley and something else to loosen that belt?

[ASSISTANT]
Ah, the classic “Honda Odyssey tensioner bolt decided to ruin your day” moment. Lovely.  

Here’s the deal:  
- **No**, don’t jam a breaker bar *against* the pulley like it’s a medieval torture device. The pulley itself isn’t designed to take torque like that—it’ll either chip, crack, or chew up the bearing. Then you’ll have *two* problems instead of one.  
- What you want is to relieve the tension *through the tensioner arm*, not by fighting the pulley bolt. On that 3.5L V6, there’s a square hole (3/8" or 1/2" drive, depending on version) in the tensioner arm itself. Stick your ratchet or breaker bar in there and rotate the whole arm to release tension. That’s the safe, intended move.  
- If the bolt that holds the pulley to the tensioner arm is stripped, you don’t need it out just to release the belt—only if you’re *replacing* the tensioner or pulley.  

So:  
1. Find the square drive hole on the tensioner body (not the bolt).  
2. Use the right-length bar—counterclockwise usually relieves tension.  
3. Slide the belt off, release tension *slowly*.  

If that bolt really is toast and you *do* need it off, you’re looking at one of these: bolt extractor socket, left-hand drill bit, or the “cut a slot with a Dremel and pray” method.  

You want to tell me if the head’s rounded, seized, or sheared? I can give you the least miserable removal method for your specific flavor of disaster.

Yeah, that poor bastard of a bolt is *properly* rounded. Whoever last touched that tensioner must’ve been using a socket made out of cheese.  

Here’s the straight-up plan before you break something expensive:  

**1. Stop prying on the pulley.**  
That tensioner arm’s pivot is aluminum. You’ll just warp it or pop the bearing. It’s not a pry bar point—it’s a spring-loaded arm meant to rotate.  

**2. Use the ½" square hole on the arm itself.**  
Look dead-center under or just beside that ruined 19 mm bolt head—there’s a recessed square drive hole cast into the tensioner bracket. You can jam a ½" breaker bar straight into that and rotate the *whole arm* clockwise (from the perspective in your video). That’s how you actually relieve the belt.  

**3. If the square hole is too packed with crud to see:**  
Hit it with brake cleaner and a pick, because that pocket fills with grime and people miss it all the time. It’s there—you just can’t see it under the filth and sadness.  

**4. Once the belt’s off, deal with the stripped bolt.**  
If you’re replacing the tensioner anyway:  
- Whack on a slightly undersized *6-point* socket (like a hammered-on 18 mm) or an extractor socket.  
- If that fails, weld a nut to the head—heat will help break it free too.  
- Worst case: pull the whole tensioner bracket (three bolts into the block) and fight it on the bench with an impact and swearing.  

You’re not crazy—this design sucks. But if you get your breaker bar in that square hole, you’ll have the belt off in thirty seconds and your sanity back in forty-five.

On the **’08**, that tensioner setup is nearly identical: you’ve still got that cursed 19 mm hex on the pulley, but it’s **not** the tension-release point. It’s just the pulley bolt. The square-drive hole is still on the tensioner arm—only difference is, on that year it’s sometimes at an awkward downward angle, hiding under road grime like it’s ashamed of existing.  

So yeah, same advice stands:  
- Don’t fight the bolt. It’s only there to hold the pulley on.  
- Clean out the tensioner arm near that bolt—use a flashlight and a pick until you uncover a ½-inch square hole.  
- Stick a long breaker bar in there and rotate *clockwise* (facing the engine). That’ll drop the tension so you can pull the belt.  

If you still can’t find that square drive, I’ll bet it’s on the underside of the arm, facing a bit toward the frame rail—Honda loved hiding it there like some kind of cruel joke. Want me to tell you the exact arm angle and direction to push once you find it?
