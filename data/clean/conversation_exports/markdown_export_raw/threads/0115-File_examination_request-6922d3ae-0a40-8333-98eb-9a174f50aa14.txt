## WHAT IS A FATAL FLAW?

A **fatal flaw** is the character's core weakness that:
1. Drives their most catastrophic decisions
2. Prevents them from achieving goals easily
3. Causes collateral damage to others
4. Escalates across Turnings (doesn't improve without intervention)
5. Often proves fatal (literally or metaphorically)

**Important:** Not all flaws kill directly, but all prove costly.

# Soul Resonance

Soulpulse Confluence is a gear-punk, grimdark fantasy universe where the fabric of existence hums with living vibration.  
Every being carries a Soulpulse Frequency—the personal thrum of its essence.  
Beneath that personal music lies the World Spine Resonance, an endless, low note that binds machinery, nature, and souls.  
By bringing their own frequency into confluence with this world resonance, resonants can influence machines, bodies, and even emotions.

## Core Laws of Magic
1. **Soulpulse Frequency** – Every sentient creature has a unique, measurable frequency. Emotions, experiences, and faith modulate this pulse.  
2. **World Spine Resonance** – An omnipresent hum that ties matter and spirit together. When the World Spine is disrupted (by Rot, grief, or excess power), reality becomes brittle.  
3. **Confluence** – When a resonant matches their pulse with the World Spine, they can influence machines, bodies, and emotions. The closer the match, the greater the effect—and the greater the cost.  
4. **Tether IDs** – Deep resonance alterations create metaphysical tethers linking resonants and others. If the resonant falls or dies, tethered beings risk collapse.  
5. **Cost** – Confluence is transactional: blood, memory, accelerated aging, emotional blunting—each act demands a price.

# Resonance Forms

Resonance Forms are distinct transformations marking a resonant’s evolution.

## First Resonance (Awakening)
- **Trigger:** Accepting true nature.  
- **Changes:** Aura visible, enhanced abilities.  
- **Power:** 2–3x human. Hours.  
- **Cost:** Fatigue, vulnerability.  
- **Social Impact:** Marks Awakened.

## Second Resonance (Integration)
- **Trigger:** Overcome deep trauma.  
- **Changes:** Glowing tattoos, energy form.  
- **Power:** 10–15x human. Minutes–hour.  
- **Cost:** Heavy drain, addiction risk.  
- **Social Impact:** Inspires awe/fear.

## Third Resonance (Sacrifice)
- **Trigger:** Sacrifice something precious.  
- **Changes:** Transcendence, reality-bending.  
- **Power:** 50–100x human. Minutes only.  
- **Cost:** Permanent soul/personality damage.  
- **Social Impact:** Legendary status.

## Ascended Form (Balance)
- **Trigger:** Perfect Nexus balance.  
- **Changes:** Perfected divine human form.  
- **Power:** 500x human. Sustainable.  
- **Cost:** Must maintain balance.  
- **Social Impact:** Worshipped.

## Divine Resonance (Transcendence)
- **Trigger:** Ego death and cosmic rebirth.  
- **Changes:** Pure energy, any form.  
- **Power:** Reality-altering godhood. Permanent.  
- **Cost:** Loss of individuality.  
- **Social Impact:** Becomes a force of nature.

# The Four Divine Principles

The Divine Principles are the opposing forces born from the First Spark, the first wound of the universe. Their friction fuels existence.

## Form (Xilcore)
- Governs structure, pattern, permanence.  
- **God:** Xilcore, pure unbending order, logic. His extremes create chaos.  
- **Manifestations:** Crystal shards, geometric light, lattices. Machinery obeys.  
- **Costs:** Body ossifies, joints stiffen, emotions rigid. Risk of shattering.

## Wholeness (Leesa)
- Governs unity, harmony, completion.  
- **Goddess:** Leesa, born of symmetry, friction-smoothing unity. Risks stifling growth.  
- **Manifestations:** Harmonic ribbons, soft light, choral tones. Wounds heal.  
- **Costs:** Pain of others becomes theirs; individuality dissolves.

- Governs cleansing, refinement, clarity.  
- **God:** Blemo, crystallized from laws of Xilcore’s star. Purges flaws blindly.  
- **Manifestations:** White light, heat, pristine aura. Souls scoured clean.  
- **Costs:** Burns indiscriminately; memory, empathy, humanity consumed.

## Decay (Seeri)
- Governs entropy, corrosion, destruction.  
- **Goddess:** Seeri, decay of perfection, entropy incarnate. Necessary but consuming.  
- **Manifestations:** Rust, ash, collapse. Bonds and structures unravel.  
- **Costs:** Body ages, sanity frays, despair consumes.

Vein Craft and Resonance (Magic System)
The “magic” of this world is deeply integrated with its physics and industry. It is often called Resonance or
simply vein craft, and it revolves around manipulating the invisible energy threads (veins) that course
through matter. Unlike traditional spellcasting with incantations or musical charms, resonance in Te’Oga is a
physical, vibration-based energy manipulation – more akin to a form of engineering or chi energy control
than wizardry . Practitioners (Veinwrights and others with talent) sense the throbbing pulse of energy
(called threft) within materials and structures, and they exert their will and skill to redirect or amplify those
forces.
Mechanics of Resonance: All matter has these veins of stress and energy, and by touching a material and
focusing, a resonant worker can inject their own energy and frequency into it. In practical terms, a resonant
pushes life-force or Aetheric energy from their body into the object, causing vibrations that either reinforce
or alter the object’s structural integrity . The effect is described in tactile, mechanical terms: for example,
energy might build in one’s chest, radiate down the arms, and bleed into metal through the palms .

because on an atomic level, it is being coerced by vibrational pressure. This is why musical metaphors are
deliberately avoided: it’s not about “singing” to the material or charming it with melody, but pressurizing it
with raw force until it yields or aligns. Indeed, within the canon of this saga, any language of music is

Costs and Limits: Using resonance is physically taxing. Every time a character pushes energy into the
environment, there is always a cost to their body . Common side effects after a heavy veinworking
session include: a coppery taste in the mouth, ears ringing, hands trembling uncontrollably, skin blistering
or cracking along energy channels, dizziness or vertigo, and sheer exhaustion that can verge on blacking

tolerance before hitting their limit. The greater the range, duration, or complexity of a resonance use, the
heavier the toll exacted . For instance, sending energy across a town’s water pipeline system for hours is
far more draining than fixing a single pump beside you for a minute. Working in tandem with another (like
Jhace and Tiffani often do in the story) can share the burden of the strain, but it never eliminates it . The
physicality of resonance keeps the magic grounded: after any intense scene of using their Veinwright
abilities, characters will be shown panting, bleeding from the nose, coughing, or experiencing some visceral
consequence.

## 1. VEIN SUBSTRATE: THE INVISIBLE STRESS ARCHITECTURE

### What Are Veins?

**Veins** are invisible channels of structural stress that exist within all materials (objects, living tissue, the planetary substrate itself).

**Pre-Piercing state:** Veins exist but are **inert**—stress channels locked in place, unresponsive to will or intention.

**Post-Piercing state:** The projectile's alien materials cause **vein-infection**—veins throughout Te'Oga become **responsive to conviction and Soulpulse manipulation**.

### Vein States: Three Conditions

Every vein exists in one of three states:

#### 1. Stable

**Definition:** Veins in equilibrium; stress distributed normally; material/body functioning as intended.

**Indicators:** No sensations; object holds; body healthy; no tremor or pain.

**Manipulation:** Stable veins are difficult to deliberately activate; require high conviction and precise application.

**Narrative use:** Characters describe stable objects as "holding" or "settled"; bodies feel "intact" or "whole".

#### 2. Stressed

**Definition:** Veins under unusual pressure; stress accumulating; material/body approaching failure point.

**Indicators:** Humming sensation; pressure-feeling (like water-weight); localized pain; tremor in proximate tissue.

**Manipulation:** Stressed veins are easiest to manipulate; they respond to conviction quickly; cost is moderate.

**Narrative use:** Characters describe stressed objects as "creaking" or "singing" (forbidden: "humming like a tuning fork"); bodies feel "stretched" or "tight".

#### 3. Rupturing

**Definition:** Veins in cascading failure; stress exceeding maximum threshold; material/body approaching structural collapse.

**Indicators:** Intense pain; visible cracks appearing; explosive sensation (pressure-release imminent); nosebleeds if in living tissue.

**Manipulation:** Rupturing veins cannot be stabilized through normal Soulpulse (cost too high; would kill user); can only be managed through Wholeness-braiding (accepting the failure and integrating around it) or accelerated through Entropy (allowing graceful collapse).

**Narrative use:** Characters describe rupturing objects as "screaming" (forbidden: "singing like a broken instrument"); bodies feel "shattering" or "dissolving from inside".

**Narrative consequence:** Magic is hardest (costliest) in Grindheim; easiest in periphery; fluctuates unpredictably in Curtain zone.

## 2. WHAT SOULPULSE IS (HARD DEFINITION)

### Core Mechanism

**Soulpulse** is the deliberate manipulation of vein state through focused conviction and directed internal energy.

**Process:**
1. User senses vein(s) in a material or living tissue
2. User focuses conviction on specific vein target
3. User channels internal energy (metabolic, emotional, or both) into the target vein
4. Vein responds by shifting state (stable → stressed, stressed → rupturing, or reversed)

## 3. ACCESS REQUIREMENT: CONVICTION

### What Is Conviction?

**Conviction** is the triple-state psychological condition required to activate Soulpulse:

#### Element 1: Certainty

**Definition:** Absolute clarity about intended outcome; no ambiguity or hesitation.

**Examples of sufficient certainty:**
- "I will stabilize this child's fractured ribs" (specific, measurable)
- "I will heal the poison in this person's blood" (specific target, clear method)
- "I will strengthen this scaffold until it holds for one hour" (time-bound, specific)

**Examples of insufficient certainty:**
- "I hope I can fix this" (hope is not certainty)
- "Maybe I can stop the bleeding" (doubt present)
- "I'll try to make it better" (vague intention)

**Consequence of insufficient certainty:** Manipulation fails; veins do not respond; no cost incurred but no benefit either.

#### Element 2: Grounding

**Definition:** Emotional stability and presence; ability to remain focused despite pain, fear, or external crisis.

**Grounding sources:**
- Love/attachment (protection of someone you love grounds you in purpose)
- Competence confidence (mastery in your craft grounds you in capability)
- Physical anchoring (feet on ground, breath controlled, body present)
- Community/crew (feeling part of something larger grounds you in collective purpose)

**Threats to grounding:**
- Panic (disassociates you from body/intention)
- Grief (overwhelms focus with emotion)
- Physical injury (pain pulls attention away)
- Doubt about worthiness (moral questioning of whether action is right)

**Consequence of insufficient grounding:** Manipulation fails or causes **backlash**—veins respond incorrectly; user experiences intense pain or temporary paralysis.

#### Element 3: Cost Acceptance

**Definition:** Conscious psychological acceptance of the permanent toll the manipulation will exact.

**What acceptance means:**
- Not intellectually knowing the cost, but *emotionally accepting* it
- Willingness to trade your physical health for the outcome
- Pre-commitment to living with the scar (literal tremor, chronic pain, sensory loss)

**How acceptance fails:**
- User hesitates mid-manipulation (thinking "this will hurt too much")
- User second-guesses the worthiness of outcome (thinking "am I really willing to sacrifice for this?")
- User tries to minimize cost through technique (thinking "I can do this efficiently"; magic doesn't work that way)

**Consequence of insufficient acceptance:** Manipulation fails at critical moment; partial vein activation causes **Hollowing cascade** (see §7).

### Why Conviction Is Difficult

Only ~33% of vein-sensitive people can achieve full conviction under pressure:

- **Certainty failure (most common):** In chaos, people become uncertain; uncertainty blocks manipulation
- **Grounding failure (second most common):** Panic/fear/grief disconnects people from body and intention
- **Cost acceptance failure (rarest but most dangerous):** People flinch from toll; backlash can cause death

**Writer-facing rule:**
- Characters using Soulpulse must consciously demonstrate all three elements
- Cannot skip through them or assume automatic success
- Failure of any element = failed manipulation or backlash

## 1. New canon: Resonance Erosion (“Spalling”)

Formal name: **Resonance Erosion**  
Street/guild slang: **spalling / spalled / spall marks**

Concept:

> Soulpulse doesn’t burn veins. It **sandblasts your pattern**. First it strips away surface stuff (hair, lashes, brows, nails). Then it chews on short-term memory and sensory clarity. If you keep pushing, it erodes identity and conviction until you Hollow.

### Stages (writer-facing ladder)

You can paste this into any system doc as the canonical definition.

```md
### Resonance Erosion (“Spalling”) — Cost Ladder

**Stage 0 – Intact**

- Hair, brows, lashes: normal.
- Senses: normal; no static or after-images.
- Mind: clean continuity of memory; no gaps.
- Status: no discernible Soulpulse use beyond ambient.

---

**Stage 1 – Singe**

- Hair: faint loss of color in streaks; tips go dry and brittle; first gray or pale strands.
- Brows/lashes: slightly thinner; fall out faster than normal.
- Skin/nails: nails chip more easily; skin at fingertips and scalp feels dry or tight.
- Senses: post-use mild ringing, light visual snow, metallic taste.
- Mind: brief “what was I just doing?” lapses after heavy work, but they recover with rest.
- Narrative: early users; normal people can hide it with a hat and a nap.

---

**Stage 2 – Shear**

- Hair: patchy thinning, especially at crown and temples; handfuls come away after big pushes.
- Brows/lashes: noticeably sparse; eyes look “too exposed.”
- Skin/nails: nails flake like cooled slag; scalp gets tension headaches and itchy dryness.
- Senses: regular static—ringing in ears, ghost after-images, phantom warmth/cold.
- Mind: short-term memory glitches (wrong names, wrong sequence of events, repeated questions); relies on notes/tokens.
- Narrative: clearly “a Veinwright who’s paid too much”; spalling is visible on sight.

---

**Stage 3 – Drift**

- Hair: mostly gone; scalp and brows bare or in scattered islands; fine body hair mostly vanished.
- Skin: faint, irregular sheen or heat-haze shimmer across scalp and forearms when resonating.
- Senses: time skips, momentary deafness or tunnel vision under stress; more frequent phantom smells/touches.
- Mind: serious continuity problems—scrambled sequences around traumatic events (especially the Piercing), misremembered conversations, false memories that feel real.
- Conviction: harder to sustain clean conviction; they cling to written anchors and other people’s reminders to hold a coherent self.
- Narrative: this is the **danger zone**; one big overextension from here risks Hollowing.

---

**Stage 4 – Hollowing (“Blank”)**

- Hair/skin: fully hairless; skin permanently dry and ash-creased at joints; no more visible progression (the body has given everything it can).
- Senses: either dulled (numb, muffled) or flooded with chaotic noise; no stable baseline.
- Mind: identity erosion. They lose the ability to trust their own memory or intentions; may forget loved ones, core oaths, or why they started fighting.
- Conviction: cannot form or hold conviction cleanly. Choices feel remote or meaningless.
- Narrative: mechanically alive, but functionally “gone.” In story terms, this is a terminal failure state unless reversed by god-tier intervention (and even then, reversal is partial at best).
```

We keep the term **Hollowing** but redefine it as Stage 4 of Erosion: not “your veins rotted,” but “your pattern has been eaten away.”

```md
## 5. COST ARCHITECTURE: WHAT MAGIC DEMANDS

### Overview

Every act of Soulpulse carves at the user. The system does not “burn veins”; it **erodes the pattern** of the person pushing power.

This erosion is called **Resonance Erosion** (guild slang: “spalling”). It progresses in stages (see Resonance Erosion ladder) and always trends one direction: from intact toward Hollow.

No use is free. The only questions are:
- *How much did this push cost?*
- *Where did the erosion land—body, senses, or self?*

### Cost Types

Costs fall into three overlapping categories:

1. **Surface Erosion (visible markers)**  
   - Hair loss: streaking → thinning → patchy → full loss (scalp, brows, lashes, body hair).  
   - Keratin damage: nails chip, flake, and eventually become thin and glass-fragile.  
   - Skin wear: dryness, cracking at joints and scalp; later a faint heat-haze shimmer when resonating.

2. **Sensory Noise (nervous system wear)**  
   - Acute: ringing in ears, metallic taste, visual snow, phantom warmth/cold, “buzz” in teeth.  
   - Chronic: time-skip flashes, tunnel vision, transient deafness, persistent ghost after-images under stress.

3. **Pattern Drift (memory + identity erosion)**  
   - Early: lost minutes after heavy use, forgetting recent steps in a repair, minor misnamings.  
   - Mid: wrong sequence for major events (especially around the Piercing), false memories that feel true, dependence on external anchors (notes, tokens, other people).  
   - Late: core identity fractures—forgetting loved ones, oaths, reasons; inability to hold conviction.

### Per-Use Cost Rules

- **Magnitude** of effect (how big the change is) increases erosion **one stage** if it exceeds the user’s current safe bandwidth.
- **Range** (how far they push beyond touch) multiplies cost by ×2 for every full body-length past direct contact (up to system hard limits in §2).
- **Duration** (how long they sustain) compounds cost: sustained use inflicts one additional category of erosion (surface + sensory, or surface + pattern).
- **Dual-principle use** applies *all* relevant costs simultaneously and always risks an immediate jump toward **Drift** or **Hollow**.

### Recovery Rules

- Surface erosion (hair, nails, minor skin dryness) can partially recover between uses at **Stage 1 (Singe)** if the user rests for weeks and avoids Soulpulse.
- Once a user reaches **Shear** (Stage 2), hair and keratin recovery is **partial and cosmetic only**; underlying sensitivity remains degraded.
- Sensory noise can ebb between casts, but a baseline “static floor” rises with every serious overuse.
- Pattern drift is **cumulative and mostly irreversible**—memories do not return cleanly. People can be reminded of facts, but continuity never feels the same.
- Hollowing (Stage 4) is functionally terminal. Even divine-level intervention may only restore fragments; the person who existed before is gone.

### Hollowing Link

Hollowing is no longer visualized as “drained veins.” Hollowing is **the endpoint of Resonance Erosion**: a pattern so carved out that conviction cannot anchor.

See §7 for mechanical details.
```

```md
## 7. HOLLOWING: TERMINAL FAILURE STATE

### Definition

**Hollowing** is the final stage of Resonance Erosion (Stage 4). It is not fatigue, not simple burnout, and not emotional numbness. It is a structural failure of the self-pattern.

A Hollowed person:
- May still breathe, walk, and respond to stimuli.
- Cannot form or sustain the triple-condition of Conviction (certainty, grounding, cost acceptance).
- Experiences their own memories as unreliable fragments, like stories told about someone else.

In setting terms: a Hollowed Veinwright is as good as dead for Soulpulse. In thematic terms: they have lost the ability to choose in any meaningful way.

### How Hollowing Happens

Hollowing is the result of **cascaded Erosion**, usually via:

1. **Repeated overuse without recovery**  
   - User rides the line between Drift and Hollow, taking “one more job” each time.
   - Surface and sensory costs are ignored or normalized until pattern drift spikes.

2. **Single catastrophic overextension (Fathombreak/trauma event)**  
   - Massive Conviction spike (e.g., grief, terror, or rage) fuels a disproportionate Soulpulse output.
   - Erosion jumps multiple stages at once; user goes from Shear → Hollow in one scene.

3. **Dual-principle abuse**  
   - Extended use of two opposing Principles forces the pattern to hold incompatible shapes.
   - The mind “lets go” rather than continue mediating contradictions.

### Symptoms

Pre-Hollow (late Drift):

- Cannot remember entire days around major events (especially the Piercing).
- Confuses identities (e.g., swaps faces/names of key people).
- Uses notebooks, tattoos, or ritual scripts to function day-to-day.
- Reports feeling “thin,” “unreal,” or “out of sync” with their own life.

Hollow:

- Flat affect or inappropriate affect (laughing at grief, blank in crisis).
- Conviction attempts fail: they try to pulse, but veins do not respond—**not** due to lack of power, but lack of cohesive self.
- Often expresses indifference to survival or outcome.

### Recovery Possibility

Hard rules:

- **No natural recovery** from Hollowing. Sleep, food, and time do not restore pattern integrity.
- Minor functional improvement is possible with external scaffolding (community retelling their story, written records, Principle-specific divine intervention), but:
  - Old memories return as static, not lived experience.
  - Conviction remains fragile; risk of collapse on any new overuse is extreme.

Writer-facing:

- Treat Hollowing as irreversible for 99% of characters.
- If any partial “recovery” happens, it must carry a cost bigger than the original Hollowing (e.g., someone else pays, a god rewrites them, etc.).
```

## 2. TEO’GA: PHYSICAL PIERCE EVENT

This section defines the physical reality of the Piercing and its immediate planetary effects.

### 2.1 The Projectile

- Origin: **alien**; not created by any known culture on Te’Oga.  
- Composition: dense, glass-like material with unknown internal structure.  
- Shape: elongated shard with a pointed leading edge and a tapering body.  
- Size: large enough that its passage opens a tunnel through the planet that is wide enough to see daylight from orbit when looking along the path.

The projectile travels on a straight path with significant momentum. Te’Oga’s gravity and mass do not deflect it enough to change that path in a meaningful way.

### 2.2 Impact Geometry

- Entry hemisphere: **northern**.  
- Entry latitude: high northern band, in a region that will later matter for story (e.g., near a major population or infrastructure node).  
- Exit latitude: near the **equator** on the opposite side of the planet.  
- The projectile does **not** pass through the geometric center of the planet. It is offset from the rotational axis.

Result:

- The projectile enters the crust in the northern hemisphere.  
- It passes through mantle and core envelope along a straight line.  
- It exits through the crust near the equator on the far side.  
- It continues into space and does not remain inside Te’Oga.

The open tunnel left behind is lined with alien residue and altered planetary material.

### 2.3 Axial Reorientation

Before the Piercing:

- Te’Oga has a non-zero axial tilt that produces distinct seasons (similar in scale to Earth-like tilt, exact number can be set later but stays in the 20–30 degree range).

After the Piercing:

- Te’Oga’s axis is nearly upright (residual tilt less than 2 degrees).  
- Long-term climate and weather bands are permanently altered.  
- Old navigation and seasonal expectations are no longer accurate.

### 2.4 Seismic Consequences

- A global series of large earthquakes as the crust and mantle respond to the new stress distribution.  
- Fault lines slip in multiple stages, not in a single clean event.  
- Surface structures feel both vertical and lateral motion.

## 3. SOULPULSE: POST-PIERCING MAGIC SYSTEM

Soulpulse does not exist in any usable form before the Piercing.  
The Piercing and its residue create the conditions for Soulpulse for the first time in Te’Oga’s history.

### 3.1 Emergence

Causal chain:

1. The projectile passes through Te’Oga and deposits trace foreign material along its path.  
2. This material interacts with existing planetary fields and structures.  
3. A new, stable resonance field forms, linked to Te’Oga’s mass and this residue.  
4. Human beings become capable of interacting with this field under specific mental and physical conditions.

From the character viewpoint in N01:

- They do not know any of this.  
- They only see impossible events and failures when their emotional and physical states align with these new conditions.

### 3.2 Definition

**Soulpulse** is the interaction between a person’s will and the planetary resonance field.  
When a person meets the conditions and focuses their intent, they can cause a controlled change in physical reality by pushing through this field.

Core requirements:

- Focused intent.  
- Emotional stability or intensity that aligns with that intent.  
- A willingness, even unconscious, to accept physical and mental cost.

These three conditions exist before the Piercing but do nothing. After the Piercing, they enable Soulpulse when the field is present.

### 3.3 Principles (High-Level)

By the end of the saga, Soulpulse is understood to express through four main behavioral patterns. Characters do not know these names or details at the start.

Principles:

- **Form**: stability, reinforcement, structure.  
- **Wholeness**: repair, integration, continuity.  
- **Purity**: focus, isolation, clarity.  
- **Entropy**: release, collapse, unbinding.

These four patterns describe how Soulpulse behaves when guided by different types of intent.

In early volumes:

- Characters see only isolated, confusing results.  
- They do not classify these behaviors correctly.  
- They cannot yet separate these patterns from luck or coincidence.

### 3.4 Visibility

Soulpulse has direct physical effects. It is not subtle in its outcomes. However, the process is not visually flashy by default.

Typical signs during use:

- Change in local air pressure.  
- Minor distortion around the user or target area.  
- Audible creak, crack, or shift if structures are involved.

There are no mandatory color glows, no mandatory visible energy beams, and no anatomical channels.

### 3.5 Cost Model

Soulpulse does not cause rot, organ damage, or permanent physical mutation as a direct effect.

The cost is **strain and exhaustion**:

#### Stage 1 — Strain

- Slight increase in breathing rate.  
- Mild muscle tension.  
- Light tremor in hands or fingers.  
- Slight headache or pressure behind the eyes.

Duration: short. Recovery: minutes with rest.

#### Stage 2 — Fatigue

- Noticeable tremor in hands and arms.  
- General muscle fatigue.  
- Difficulty maintaining focus.  
- Irritability or emotional volatility.

Duration: hours. Recovery: several hours or a night of sleep.

#### Stage 3 — Burnout

- Full-body exhaustion.  
- Strong tremors that interfere with precise tasks.  
- Dizziness on standing or during movement.  
- Trouble organizing thoughts or making decisions.

Duration: a day or more. Recovery: multiple nights of sleep, food, and reduced activity.

#### Stage 4 — Collapse

- Body cannot remain standing.  
- User may lose consciousness or enter a near-unresponsive state.  
- Breathing and vital signs remain stable unless external harm occurs.  
- Soulpulse use is not possible until at least partial recovery.

Duration: days. Recovery: days to weeks, depending on severity and support.

### 3.6 Fatality

Soulpulse does not directly kill the user through its internal mechanics.

Death can occur only when:

- A user collapses in a dangerous environment (falling structures, fire, drowning, etc.).  
- A user pushes past physical limits while performing an extreme act (for example, trying to hold up a collapsing structure beyond any reasonable capacity).

In these cases, the cause of death is environmental or mechanical failure, not an internal magical effect.

### 3.7 Recovery and Assistance

Recovery methods:

- Rest and sleep.  
- Food and water.  
- Safe, low-stress environment.  
- Emotional support and grounding.

In later volumes:

- Healing applications of Soulpulse can assist recovery from fatigue and burnout, but using Soulpulse to accelerate recovery still consumes energy and must be applied by a second user or under tightly controlled conditions.

### 3.8 Early-Stage Failures (N01 Emphasis)

Because Soulpulse is new:

- Early users do not know the safe limits.  
- They do not understand the cost curve.  
- They do not understand the correlation between emotional state and outcome.

Common failure modes in N01:

- Attempting to stabilize a structure and causing an unexpected collapse.  
- Losing consciousness at a critical moment due to unrecognized strain.  
- Triggering a small effect unintentionally during a moment of stress, causing confusion or injury.  
- Repeated attempts that produce no effect, followed by one sudden, uncontrolled success when strain is already high.

The tone in early volumes is dark and grounded. Soulpulse feels dangerous and unreliable. The world treats it as a threat and a tool at the same time.

### 3.9 Knowledge Progression Across 30 Volumes

High-level:

- N01–N05: instinct, accidents, improvised tricks. No formal structure.  
- N06–N10: early theory, guilds or groups start to track patterns.  
- N11–N20: codified principles and technical training.  
- N21–N30: deep understanding of the global system, long-range consequences, and ethical limits.

At the end of the saga:

- Characters have a clear model of Soulpulse and its costs.
