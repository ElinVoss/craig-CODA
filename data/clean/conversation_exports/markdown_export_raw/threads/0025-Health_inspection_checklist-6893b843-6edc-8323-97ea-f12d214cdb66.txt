Here's a **comprehensive health inspection checklist** tailored for a **kitchen inside a bowling center in Alexandria, Louisiana**, based on **Louisiana Department of Health (LDH)** food safety regulations and general food code practices.

---

## ✅ Health Inspection Preparation Checklist  
### 📍 For a Bowling Center Kitchen — Alexandria, LA

---

### 🔹 1. **Licensing & Documentation**
- [ ] Current LDH food establishment permit is posted and valid.  
- [ ] Employee food handler certifications and manager food safety certifications are up to date and available.  
- [ ] Pest control records available and up to date.  
- [ ] Most recent health inspection report on file.

---

### 🔹 2. **Employee Hygiene & Practices**
- [ ] Employees are wearing clean uniforms, hair restraints (hats, hairnets, beard nets).  
- [ ] Handwashing stations fully stocked (soap, hot water, paper towels).  
- [ ] Employees wash hands properly and frequently.  
- [ ] No eating, drinking (except closed beverage containers), or smoking in food prep areas.  
- [ ] Sick employees are not working (illness reporting policy in place).

---

### 🔹 3. **Food Handling & Storage**
- [ ] All food stored at least 6 inches off the floor.  
- [ ] Food is covered, labeled, and dated.  
- [ ] No expired or spoiled food present.  
- [ ] Raw meat stored below ready-to-eat foods.  
- [ ] Thermometers present in all refrigerators/freezers and calibrated.  
- [ ] Refrigerated food at or below 41°F; hot food at or above 135°F.

---

### 🔹 4. **Cooking, Cooling, Reheating**
- [ ] Cooking temperatures meet food safety standards (e.g., poultry 165°F).  
- [ ] Food is cooled properly (135°F to 70°F in 2 hours, then to 41°F in 4 more).  
- [ ] Reheated foods reach 165°F for 15 seconds.  
- [ ] Microwave reheating procedures documented (if applicable).

---

### 🔹 5. **Cleaning & Sanitizing**
- [ ] 3-compartment sink is set up correctly: wash, rinse, sanitize.  
- [ ] Proper sanitizer concentration (chlorine: 50-100 ppm; quats: 200-400 ppm).  
- [ ] Test strips available and used regularly.  
- [ ] All food-contact surfaces cleaned and sanitized regularly.  
- [ ] Cutting boards, knives, utensils, etc., in good condition and sanitized.  

---

### 🔹 6. **Equipment & Facility Maintenance**
- [ ] Equipment clean, in good repair, and functioning (coolers, fryers, etc.).  
- [ ] Hood and ventilation systems clean and grease-free.  
- [ ] Lights have shields or shatter-proof bulbs.  
- [ ] Floors, walls, and ceilings are clean and in good condition.  
- [ ] No standing water, leaks, or excessive grease buildup.  

---

### 🔹 7. **Pest Control**
- [ ] No signs of pests (rodents, insects, droppings).  
- [ ] Doors and windows have screens or self-closers.  
- [ ] Pest control company on regular service schedule.  

---

### 🔹 8. **Restrooms**
- [ ] Restrooms are clean, stocked, and have self-closing doors.  
- [ ] Handwashing signs posted in all restrooms.  

---

### 🔹 9. **Trash & Waste Disposal**
- [ ] Trash cans covered, leak-proof, and emptied regularly.  
- [ ] Grease traps cleaned and properly maintained.  
- [ ] Dumpster area clean and free from overflow or pests.  

---

### 🔹 10. **Special for Bowling Center**
- [ ] Snack bar and drink station areas follow the same sanitation and storage rules.  
- [ ] Any vending machines or self-serve machines are clean and regularly maintained.  
- [ ] No cross-contamination between recreational areas and food prep areas.  

---

### 📝 Tips Before Inspection Day:
- Do a mock inspection the day before.
- Make sure your team knows their roles in food safety.
- Keep all temperature logs and cleaning logs up to date.
- Greet the inspector courteously and accompany them during the inspection.
