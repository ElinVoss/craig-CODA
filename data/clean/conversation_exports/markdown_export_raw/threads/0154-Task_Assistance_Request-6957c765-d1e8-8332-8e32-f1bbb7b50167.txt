Closet office can be awesome if you treat it like a tiny workstation bay, not a “desk shoved in a hole.” Here’s a setup that works even if your closet is awkward.

## The winning priorities
1) **Ergonomics first** (monitor height, chair fit, keyboard/mouse distance)  
2) **Ventilation + heat management** (closets cook electronics)  
3) **Cable discipline** (or it becomes a rat nest instantly)  
4) **Vertical storage** (walls are your square footage)

---

## Layout options (pick one)
### Option A: “Command Shelf” (best overall)
A sturdy desktop surface + shelves above, nothing on the floor except a small drawer unit.

**How:**
- Desk surface: 20–24" deep is usually enough; width = closet width minus 1–2".
- Put monitor(s) on an arm or riser so the surface stays clear.
- Above-desk: 2 shelves (one “daily,” one “archive”).

**Why:** You can work with the door open or closed, and it stays clean.

### Option B: “Fold-Down Desk” (best if you need the closet to still be a closet)
Mount a wall-mounted folding desk or build a hinged top.

**How:**
- Desk folds down when you work.
- Peripherals live on a shelf or in bins above.

**Why:** Converts the closet back to storage instantly.

### Option C: “Sidewall Workbench” (best when closet is shallow)
If depth is tight, put the desk across the back wall and mount everything else to side walls.

**How:**
- Desk shallow (16–20" deep).
- Mount monitor, headset hook, pegboard, etc.

**Why:** Keeps knees from smashing into doors and keeps you centered.

---

## The “Closet Office Standard Build” (simple, solid, cheap-ish)
### Desk & posture
- **Chair:** your elbows should be ~90° at the keyboard. If your chair is high, use a footrest (even a solid box works).
- **Monitor:** top of screen at or slightly below eye level.
- **Keyboard/mouse:** keep them close; forearms supported if possible.

### Lighting (this makes or breaks it)
Closets are shadow caves. Fix it:
- **Under-shelf LED bar** or a **small clamp lamp** aimed at the wall (bounce light = less glare).
- If you do video calls: a small **front fill light** (cheap ring light is fine).

### Ventilation / heat (don’t ignore this)
If you run a PC/console/charging in there:
- Keep the **door cracked** when running heavy loads.
- Add a **quiet USB fan** pulling air out, or a small fan at the top shelf moving warm air out.
- Don’t trap a PC tower against the back wall—give it breathing room.

### Sound & “closet echo”
Closets can sound like you’re calling from a bathroom.
- Hang a **jacket/blanket** on one side or use a **fabric panel**. Instant improvement.

### Power + cable management (make it idiot-proof)
- Mount a **surge protector/power strip** to the wall/desk underside.
- Route all cables to one “spine” using:
  - adhesive cable clips
  - a cable sleeve
  - Velcro ties (not zip ties unless you like suffering later)
- Put chargers on a **charging shelf** so you’re not hunting cables.

### Storage (vertical)
- One shelf = “daily tools” (notebooks, headset, tablet, frequently used stuff).
- One shelf = “rarely used.”
- Add a **pegboard** or wall rail for:
  - headphones hook
  - scissors/utility knife
  - small tools
  - cords

### If you use a laptop
- Use a **laptop stand** + **external keyboard/mouse**. Otherwise your neck will hate you by Day 3.
