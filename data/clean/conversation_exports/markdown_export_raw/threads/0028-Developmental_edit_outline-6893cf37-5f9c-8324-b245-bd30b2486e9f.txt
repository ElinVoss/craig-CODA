Over‑extended roster. Some POVs (e.g., Brighlee’s full chapters or Lennox’s extended monologues) contribute color but not essential plot developments. Keep divine interludes concise and abstract. Use them at act breaks to foreshadow stakes and mirror mortal choices. Avoid giving them too much page‑time; the mortals’ story should stay primary.

Chapter ordering. Some chapters (especially Act II) spend multiple pages on philosophical digression before advancing the plot.
