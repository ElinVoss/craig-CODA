For a 3-wire alternator harness, the usual functions are:  
1. **Battery output (B+)** – large gauge feed that goes to battery positive.  
2. **Ignition/field exciter (D+ or F)** – feed from ignition switch, often via indicator lamp, that tells regulator to start generating.  
3. **Sense or monitor wire** – a smaller gauge that monitors battery voltage or alternator output, helping the regulator adjust.

For your engine (2006, 4.0 L), the factory plug with solid yellow, red/green stripe, and yellow/green stripe likely corresponded to those three functions. According to forum posts and wiring notes:  
- The “charge” cable from the alternator B+ terminal runs to the positive battery terminal. citeturn0search4turn0search2  
- The exciter/ignition feed is separate and smaller gauge.  
- The sense wire monitors voltage.

### ✅ What to do with your new plug (all black wires)  
Since the colours are gone, you’ll need to determine pin positions. Here’s how to proceed:

1. **Find a factory wiring diagram** for the exact alternator model & vehicle. This will show which pin (A, B, C or 1,2,3) goes to which function.

2. **Identify by function rather than colour**:  
   - Locate the **thickest wire** in the plug/harness: that is very likely the B+ output to battery.  
   - The smallest gauge wire in the plug is probably the **sense** or **ignition feed**.  
   - The remaining mid-sized wire is likely the **exciter/field** feed.

3. **Trace physically**:  
   - Use a continuity tester or voltmeter. With battery connected and vehicle off, trace which wire goes to battery positive (or fuse/relay near battery).  
   - Verify the exciter wire is hot only with ignition on (key turned to run).  
   - The sense wire will be tied into the battery positive line or main wiring harness and often connected at the fuse box.

4. **Label as you go**: Once identified, mark each wire in your new harness: “B+”, “IGN/Excite”, “Sense”.

- Before reconnecting, **disconnect the battery negative terminal** to prevent shorts.  
- Ensure all connections are clean, tight, and use heat-shrink or dielectric grease if needed.
